# Retrospective: The Doctrine

One page, process only. Product story: the chronicle entry.

## What worked

- **The census keystone was caught at brainstorm time, not at review.**
  The ideonomy side-effect prompt surfaced that a second voice per
  culture entering `accounts_of` would silently shift every pairwise
  census dial value — before a line was written. The dial-roster law
  (separate `doctrines_of` collection + the fixture byte-identity proof)
  was designed in from the spec, and the population proof ran clean
  three times. The overlay's "run a pass on the obvious answers" rule
  paid for the whole campaign here.
- **A review-demanded edge test falsified the plan's own grid.** Writing
  the precedence test for folk-Lost vs doctrine-Explained{Lost} exposed
  that the plan's compressed Harmony condition classified *asymmetric
  explanation* as agreement; the spec's letter ("accounts differ")
  governed, the classifier was refined (explanatory parity), and the
  refinement is what later made the disclosure law's loud guard
  necessary — one small honest test pulling a thread through three
  tasks. Spec > plan is not just a tiebreak rule; it is a bug-finder.
- **Fail-loud fallback arms are now reflex** (the C5 F2 lesson's first
  full campaign): the SOC-1 negative sweep panics if reality stops
  providing folk flagships; the non-moon RevealedClaim and the
  annotation-less Contested arms panic rather than vanish; the Contested
  canary reddens the day the Galileo cell becomes real. Every "cannot
  happen today" state has a tripwire, and two of them were added
  *because* reviewers asked what happens when today ends.
- **Measured honesty over invented drama:** no real Contested claim
  exists at the floor (only moon-count can flip, and where folk keep it
  the doctrine's differing story is unverifiable-or-harmonious in
  practice) — pinned as a structural finding with synthetic proof,
  rather than padding the roster or weights to force a Galileo moment.

## What the campaign got wrong, and what it taught

- **The bash-chain trap ate a ledger entry — again.** Ledger #9's append
  sat in a `&&` chain behind a `cargo test` that failed; the write
  silently never ran, and only the final whole-branch review noticed the
  digest was missing its one post-G3 semantic refinement. The standing
  lesson (mutations get their own Bash call) exists precisely for this;
  the controller broke it under momentum. The restored entry now carries
  its own scar note.
- **The plan compressed the spec's conflict grid and the compression was
  wrong** (asymmetric explanation → Harmony). Plans that restate a spec
  table should quote it, not paraphrase it — a paraphrase is a second
  implementation of the same contract, and second implementations drift.
- **`cmd_book` was missing from the plan's Files list** for the surface
  task (T3's implementer caught that the artifact would have regenerated
  empty). The render-surface checklist for Book campaigns is now: the
  window, the CLI emitter, the artifact, the parse arm — all four, every
  time.

worlds (preregistered trade; revisit with C7's evidential frames). The

only revealable; the non-moon panic guard forces the conversation when
