# Retrospective: The Diachronic Book

One page, process only. Product story: the chronicle entry — which also
closes the program; the program-level retrospective lives in the
metaplan's completion note.

## What worked

- **The smallest plan of the program (2 tasks) because the design was
  fully convergent** — every mechanism (events, capability, doctrine,
  margins, the ladder) already existed; the campaign was one derivation
  and one section. Eight campaigns of substrate made the ceiling
  campaign nearly free — the strangler-fig sequencing paying out at the
  end exactly as the metaplan bet.
- **The dispatch brief's narrative was wrong and the implementer's
  measurement won** (the margin fires at seeds 1–3, not 2–3: seed 1's
  priesthoods miss 2,462 lunar events). Fourth campaign running where
  measure-don't-narrate caught the CONTROLLER's error — the discipline
  protects against the plan's author, which is its highest use.
- **A parked subagent was recovered by the book**: T1's implementer
  ended its turn "waiting" on a background job (the documented
  death-mode); the challenge-response caught it in one glance, the
  un-park message restored it, and the reviewer explicitly audited for
  half-finished state afterward (found none). The dispatching skill's
  red-flag table works when actually read.
- **The close absorbed a full EPOCH mid-walk** (the Rains' moisture
  budget) and the re-pin discipline held: two cults flipped folk under
  the new climate, their Reckoning lines degraded exactly as the SOC-1
  gate demands, and one test whose live premise vanished was
  strengthened (a pure predicate extracted and both arms pinned) rather
  than weakened. Three parallel-epoch absorptions in two days have made
  this routine; the runbook is: regenerate on the merged tree, diagnose
  every drift to its owning campaign, re-pin with provenance, prove the
  sweep empty.

## What the campaign got wrong, and what it taught

- **The controller shipped a wrong margin narrative in a dispatch brief**
  (asserted which seeds fire from memory instead of deriving). Cheap
  this time because the implementer measured; the lesson is standing:
  a dispatch brief may state expectations only as *predictions to
  verify*, never as facts — the C7 landscape-table idiom (measure in
  task N, hand the table to task N+1) is the right form and briefs
  should use nothing looser.
- **The tier-0 constant-sun panic** was latent in the plan (the
  Reckoning assumed a calendar); a pre-existing test caught it and the
  fix was the honest empty arm. Plans for time-parameterized features
  should name the tier-0/degenerate world in the test list explicitly.
