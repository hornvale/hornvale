# Retrospective: The Vessel Stitch

One page, process only. Product story: the chronicle entry.

## What worked

- **A campaign priced by its history.** Two tasks, six lines of consult
  assembly, one key-split adapter — because C6 and C8 each built their
  half of this seam on purpose (the reader-set type, the single-epoch
  accessor). The cheapest campaign of the year is the receipt for the
  program's architecture: followups filed as *named seams* become
  stitches, not projects.
- **T1 shrank honestly.** The spec said the accessor was "currently
  internal"; the implementer measured, found C8 had already exposed it
  and cut the CLI over, and delivered the accessor LAW plus docs instead
  of re-doing work — then flagged the spec staleness for the close
  rather than silently editing history.
- **The mutation arm is structural.** Heard≠true isn't asserted by a
  test alone: `reader_set` carries only (subject, predicate) keys, so a
  wrong written value CANNOT reach the render — the test documents a
  property the data flow enforces. The final reviewer played it and
  confirmed the surface.
- **The final review played the game** — scripted possession sessions,
  the rename fallthrough, garbage input, a hundred-year wait — and its
  findings were experiential (the dangling initiated line, the flat
  early-day reckoning), exactly what structural tests cannot see. The
  Lens campaign's lesson ("open the PNGs yourself") generalizes: play
  the build.

## What the campaign got wrong, and what it taught

- **A pre-existing Cargo.lock drift surfaced**: an earlier campaign
  added vessel deps without running `make vessel-check`, leaving the
  Casement's wasm lock stale until this campaign's gate refreshed it.
  Process note: any change to `windows/vessel/Cargo.toml` owes a
  `vessel-check` run in the same commit (the lock is committed; the
  check is the refresher).
- **The spec's "what exists" section drifted within 12 hours of being
  written** (C8 shipped the accessor between the survey and T1). In a
  multi-session repo this fast, "verified in code at ⟨commit⟩" headers
  earn their keep — and implementers re-verifying the survey (as T1 did)
  is the real defense, not fresher surveys.
