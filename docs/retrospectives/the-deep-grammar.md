# Retrospective: The Deep Grammar

One page, process only. Product story: the chronicle entry.

## What worked

- **The measured depth landscape as a blast-radius map.** T2 pinned the
  exact per-(seed, species) depth triples before T3 touched a surface;
  T3's artifact diff was then verified line-by-line against the map (5
  changed lines predicted, 5 observed; 2 shallow-identical, both
  confirmed). Declaring a non-additive blast radius at G3 and then
  *predicting it exactly* is the difference between a controlled change
  and a drift.
- **The close absorbed a moving world and the laws held.** The
  Confluence's settlement re-baseline added a kobold to seed 3 and
  flipped a hobgoblin cult to folk mid-close; the re-pins were honest
  (one appended row, one dropped arm — each with provenance), the SOC-1
  gate did exactly what its law promises, and the chorus fixture came
  back byte-identical because the parallel campaign had already
  re-baselined it. Cross-campaign artifact races are now routine to
  settle: regenerate on the merged tree, verify the drift belongs to the
  interaction, commit the truth.
- **Draw only what varies.** The notation-pass rule — evidential
  position fixed (near-universal), class position drawn (genuinely
  varies), depth drawn, values derived — kept the anti-astrology line
  crisp in both directions: no fake derivations, and no fake contingency
  either.
- **Family-cognate morphemes fell out nearly free** by reusing the
  proto/cascade pipeline (`lexicon_of`'s own resolution, verified
  line-for-line by the T2 review) — the highest-realism feature of the
  campaign cost one function.

## What the campaign got wrong, and what it taught

- **A review fix landed in code but not in prose — again, and this time
  the docs actively lied.** T1's silent-fallback-to-loud fix left three
  doc comments promising the removed fallback; the final review caught
  all three. The C6 lesson said "plans that restate spec tables drift";
  the general form is: **a fix that changes an arm's behavior owes every
  doc sentence that describes that arm, in the same commit** — grep the
  identifier before committing the fix.
- **One-syllable proto draws collide, and the ledger's first
  adjudication of it was wrong** (recorded cross-axis; measured
  same-axis: seed-1 goblin's animate == inanimate == "Wo"). The final
  review re-measured rather than trusting the note. Lesson: adjudicate
  homophony findings by *drawing the values*, not by reasoning about
  which axis they landed on. The design question (merger-aware marker
  draws vs honest syncretism) went to G6 as a presented fidelity call.
- **The accidental census-study launch** (a T1 implementer ran a banned
  live study while checking artifact freshness; it timed out harmlessly)
  — zero trace, verified twice, but the near-miss argues the preamble
  should name the *specific* census study files, not just the category.
