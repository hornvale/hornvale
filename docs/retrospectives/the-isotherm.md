# Retrospective — The Isotherm

Process lessons, not product. Campaign: five additive `scene/tiles/v1`
climate fields as cross-repo contracts, consumed by the orrery
(hornvale#2, hv#5 core).

## What worked

- **The consumer-proof-as-tracer discipline.** Scoping the orrery half to a
  single visible feature (the seasonal-ice overlay) that exercises *every*
  new contract element — both temperature layers, the hemisphere sign, the
  season phase, the freeze derivation — caught what parse-only tests never
  would have. It is also what made the fidelity honest: the overlay forced
  us to confront that seed 42 has <1° obliquity and therefore no visible
  seasons, which a schema-only campaign would never have noticed.

- **The final whole-branch review paid for itself in one finding.** Every
  per-task review was clean; the branch-scope review (dispatched on the
  most capable model, per the SDD guidance) found the equivalence test was
  circular — its golden generated by the client's own formula, not the
  producer's. That is precisely the class of defect a narrow per-task lens
  cannot see: each half was internally correct; only the *seam* was wrong.
  The lesson: on a campaign whose deliverable is a cross-repo contract, the
  broad review is not optional polish — it is the only reviewer positioned
  to check the thing the campaign is *for*.

- **Elements + a documented evaluator, over baked layers.** Reusing the
  orrery's own established idiom (its ephemeris already reconstructs the
  calendar's phases from `scene/system/v1` elements) kept the new data thin
  and honest: a swing coefficient and a period, not per-season snapshots; a
  band count, not a per-tile wind field. The precedent search paid off —
  the shape was already in the repo.

## What to carry forward

- **A cross-repo golden must be sourced from the producer, full stop.** The
  circular-test trap is seductive because the numbers are identical either
  way — the client formula and the Rust formula agree, so a golden from
  either passes. The discipline that catches it: the golden's *provenance*
  must name a producer function (here, `temperature_grid` → `temperature_at`),
  and the generator must not be reachable from the consumer's code. Bake
  this into any future scene-consumer contract test.

- **Gitignored vendored artifacts break "commit the binary" plans.** The
  plan said to commit `public/hornvale_world.wasm`; it is gitignored
  (vendored at build). Cost a mid-execution correction. When a plan touches
  another repo's build-time artifacts, check `.gitignore` during planning,
  not execution.

- **Keystone refreeze is real and it will find you at the merge.** The
  Menagerie's niche cutover regenerated the exact tiles fixtures this
  campaign appended to; the merge conflicted on both. Resolution was
  textbook (take main's base, regenerate from the merged code so the new
  fields append to the new base) — but only because the close walk's step 4
  named it in advance. The absorption was the branch's *first* meeting with
  main since it began: a shorter-lived campaign, or stage-boundary
  absorption, would have met a smaller delta. Noted per the close checklist.

## Golden-pin discipline

Held. The two tiles fixtures were rebaselined in the commits that drifted
them (Task 3), reviewed as append-only contract changes, and refrozen from
main's tip at the merge. No deferred re-pins; the gate was green at every
boundary.

## Registry

No idea-registry row shipped, spec'd, or was rejected — the campaign was
ticket-driven (hornvale#2, hv#5). The adjacent frontier rows
(CLIM-precip-units, CLIM-cryosphere, CLIM-ice-albedo) remain `raw` by
deliberate deferral: this campaign exposed the *existing* moisture/temperature
model, and explicitly did not promote it to typed precipitation or a real
ice model. Those rows are now more concretely reachable (the data is at the
boundary), but reachability is not a status flip.
