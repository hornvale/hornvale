# 0021. No alignment axis; species are not moral types

**Status:** Accepted (2026-07-07) · **Decider:** Nathan

In the context of inter-group interaction, warfare, exploitation, and
prejudice becoming modeled systems, facing the fantasy-genre default of
good-versus-evil species with baked-in moral valence, we decided that
**Hornvale has no alignment axis and no species-level moral type; a
people's conduct is a situation derived from material conditions, and any
ideology ranking species is a generated output, never an input**, accepting
that we forgo the genre's easiest source of stakes.

**Context.** The interaction layer (frontier item 9) and the inequality
seam (frontier social-structure section) model slavery, persecution,
menageries, trade, and cohabitation as consequences of niche overlap, power
asymmetry, subsistence, and psychology — quantities the sim already holds.
An `evil: bool` (or `alignment` enum) on a species would short-circuit all
of it: a future campaign could casually reintroduce authored morality and
collapse a derived system into a lookup table. This record forecloses that.

**Consequence.** No species carries a moral valence. A bugbear people
holding goblin slaves is a *regime* the same mechanics would produce with
the roles reversed under reversed conditions; peaceful cohabitation is what
low niche-overlap yields for free. Crucially, **"race" — the doctrine that
some species are inferior — is itself an output**: a legitimating myth the
subjugation regime manufactures to justify an extraction already underway
(the racecraft thesis), appended to the ledger and recorded as truth by a
biased historiography. The gap between the true ledger and the chronicle's
racecraft is the epistemic layer's most serious use. The cost is
deliberate: no cosmic villains, and stakes must be earned from history and
consequence rather than handed out by type.

**See also.** Frontier item 9 (contact and exchange) and the
social-structure inequality seam (`docs/vision/frontier.md`); the epistemic
layer (frontier item 2); decision 0016 (the Lab decomposes conflict's
resource-versus-identity terms empirically rather than asserting them).
