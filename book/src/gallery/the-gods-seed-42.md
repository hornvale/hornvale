# The Gods of Seed 42

This is the Year-1 exit demo (spec §15) — the single comparison the whole
cascade was built to make legible. The version that closed Year 1 described
the pair below as one settlement wearing two skies: the same flagship, the
same caste structure, differing only in which god presided. The ledger was
never quite that tidy, and successive campaigns have made the divergence
impossible to gloss. Firm Ground re-scored every settlement site when it made
a coastal cell's `freshwater` honest, moving the spinning world's flagship
outright; [Firm Ground II](../chronicle/campaign-y2-0.md) stood every
observation on its observer's own ground; [The Branches](../chronicle/the-branches.md)
seated two new peoples — hobgoblin and bugbear — on the same globe and
re-based every name when goblin joined a language family; and the deeper
astronomy and terrain campaigns of Year 2 — [The Long
Count](../chronicle/the-long-count.md) among them — filled the sky with
eclipses, a wanderer, and tides and re-drew the ground beneath every
settlement, so seed 42's world today holds **182** settlements under the
spinning sky and **172** under the locked one, not the few dozen this page
once counted. So the divergence is itself the finding, not a regression to
apologize for: astronomy was always going to propagate through settlement,
culture, and religion, and it propagates through *placement* too, one step
earlier in the cascade than this page used to show.

The names have moved most of all. Every earlier edition quoted names from a
six-syllable pool; [The Tongues](../chronicle/16-the-tongues.md) replaced
them with real generated sound; The Branches re-based them onto a shared
goblinoid ancestor; and, most recently, [The Speakable](../chronicle/the-speakable.md)
moved them again — this time to *repair a silence*. Before it, seed 42's
bugbear pantheon rendered every one of its gods as the single syllable
*Bvaash*, goblin's as *Neb*, hobgoblin's as *Fee*: a species whose drawn
syllable-templates rejected its own evolved words had every glossed name
crushed onto one fallback shape by a repair pass meant for foreign loans.
The fix admits a language's own words as evidence of what it may say, so a
name built from them surfaces them whole — the bugbear shadow-god, the one
that mythologizes the moon's shadow crossing the sun, is now literally
*Daoqao*, the bugbear word for that shadow, where it used to be a featureless
*Bvaash*. What follows is quoted verbatim from the committed almanacs, which
this seed always reproduces.

Read as their own settlements rather than one settlement wearing two skies,
the two flagships sharpen the comparison. Everywhere the sky reaches, the two
peoples' pantheons diverge; everywhere it does not, they converge in kind —
both net out farming a forest and both raise an organized priesthood (a
goblin `shaman` rung, a kobold `keeper` rung). What moves is exactly what
astronomy controls: whether the sun rises or hangs fixed, whether the moons
are seen at all, and — read across the two species on one globe — which of
those bodies a given pair of eyes crowns first. That is a stronger
demonstration of the enrichment thesis than the old, coincidental "same
village" framing ever was. A different seed breaks even the convergence —
seed 7, [the culture chapter](../domains/culture.md)'s exit demo, grows a
three-rung ladder under one sky and a five-rung one under the other. Seed
42's contribution is the sharper split: what the sky reaches against what it
does not.

Since [The Eyes](../chronicle/15-the-eyes.md), religion runs once per
species-flagship — each through that species' own perception lens and at its
own characteristic hour — so the single "The Gods" section holds one block
per pantheon that formed. Four peoples now means four blocks; this page's own
comparison stays with the original goblin/kobold pair its argument was built
around, since that pair alone already carries both axes — rotation and
species — this page exists to teach. The page it grew into, the same seed
with the sky held fixed and the two peoples traced apart across all of Year
2's vectors at once, is [The Meeting of Seed 42](./the-meeting-seed-42.md),
this gallery's Year-2 capstone.

The spinning sky is a busy one — the sun on its 0.88-day round, two moons,
a wanderer that returns every 386.98 days, two kinds of eclipse, and the
tides the moons raise — and every pantheon mythologizes the same inventory
of phenomena, each in its own voice and its own order. The anonymous lead
block, the identity contract's surviving trace of the pre-Eyes single-pantheon
page, now belongs to **bugbear**, the species that sorts first in the
registry, not to goblin; [Religion](../domains/religion.md) names that exact
byte-stability assumption as a standing debt, and this is the case the debt
described. Nothing broke — the attribution is simply silent.

Read the goblin and kobold blocks against each other and the species axis
appears cleanly. Both peoples see the identical sky, but they crown it
differently. **Goblin's pantheon leads with the sun** — *Noaboo the
Xngoknoaboo*, returning every 0.88 days — the day-lit body a day-tuned eye
weighs above all others. **Kobold's pantheon leads with a moon** —
*Roqrraxogrra Xogrra*, on the first moon's 15.99-day period — and demotes the
sun to fourth. The sun is not unseen by the warren; it is simply outweighed.
The arithmetic is in the sky section itself: the vast moon's raw salience is
`0.64`, and a kobold's lens discounts the day sky so heavily that the sun's
raw `1.00` falls to roughly `0.52` behind it, while a goblin's undiscounted
day sky keeps the sun on top. One sky, two lenses, two heads — the same
`Rank`-versus-`Knowledge` status split that shapes each people's caste ladder
also shaping which body each crowns and, audibly, how each speaks of it.
Read the voices: the kobold telling repeats its refrain (*"That's how it's
always been"*) twice a line and names each god's standing in words (*"is a
great one among the gods"*), where the goblin telling never repeats and marks
nothing (*"So it was, so it is"*).

Pin the same seed's rotation to tidally locked and the sun stops setting. The
identical eternal-vs-cyclic split, fed the identical phenomenon with a
different periodicity, mints an **eternal** head where the spinning sky mints
a cyclic one: goblin's *Toeteanoaboo the Vnodtoetea* no longer *returns* but
*watches unceasing*, a spirit fixed forever above the day side — the
frontier's alien-religion note, a terminator culture's theology hanging on an
unmoving sun, cashed out on the page.

Two things the spinning sky carried are simply gone here, and their absence is
the whole point. There is **no moon among the gods** — not one
`celestial-body` deity on a lunar period, where the spinning warren crowned
one first of all. A locked world's habitable cells are its day side, and the
day side never shows its people the moons as bodies to venerate; they survive
only as what the day side *can* feel of them — the tides they raise (*"felt,
not counted"*) and the eclipses that are the moon's own shadow crossing the
sun (the 71.11- and 269.79-day gods). And there is **no wandering-star deity**
either, for the same reason the wanderer never enters the day side's salient
sky at all. So the species axis that separated the two heads under the
spinning sky **collapses** here: with no moon to crown, a kobold's
night-weighted lens has nothing to lift the sun below, and both the goblin
village and the kobold warren lead with the same eternal sun. A discount can
only reorder a list; it cannot conjure a body the warren's own hemisphere
never shows it.

What the collapse does *not* erase is voice. The kobold warren's telling still
repeats and still names each god's standing in words; the goblin village's is
still terse and unmarked. The split that survives every rotation and every
seed is not which body a people crowns — that the sky can take away — but how
a people speaks of whatever it crowns.

## What this pair proves

Nothing about religion's code changed between these two worlds, and nothing
about settlement's or culture's changed either. The same placement suitability
formula, reading the same corrected freshwater field, sent the goblin and
kobold flagships to different cells because the two skies' climates disagreed
about where the best cell was; the same culture functions grew a farming
society under each; and the same religion genesis, reading each flagship's own
salience-ranked phenomena and its own society summary, produced different
pantheons because the phenomena and the society it was handed differed exactly
where the sky differed — the entire claim of the trace protocol (spec §3.1.6):
religion never learns which system produced a phenomenon, so it need not know
that astronomy exists, or that settlement moved, to mythologize correctly
whatever it is handed.

The kobold pantheon adds a second axis to the same proof. Under the spinning
sky, species alone moves the head from solar to lunar: one sky, two lenses,
two heads. Pin the rotation and both axes act at once — the sun's voice flips
from cyclic to eternal, and the day side takes the kobold's moon away
entirely, collapsing both peoples' heads onto the one body a fixed day still
shows. What survives that collapse is voice: the same repeating, rank-naming
telling against the same terse, unmarked one. Ask each world's leading god
*why* it is what it is — `why <id>` in the REPL, backed by
`windows/historiography::recount` — and the answers cite the same predicate
vocabulary against genuinely different phenomena: one points at a body that
departs and returns, another at one that never has and never will.

This is the composition of many campaigns' worth of already-working parts, not
a new invention: astronomy drew the rotation regime, climate reorganized its
bands, terrain's globe never moved, settlement placed a flagship the
reorganized climate preferred, culture grew a society from whatever ground
that flagship landed on, religion mythologized whatever phenomena stood at the
top of the list, and language — reading only that flagship's own species and
each belief's own facts — gave the result a name and a voice, in the species'
own evolved words, that neither religion nor settlement had to know anything
about. See [Religion](../domains/religion.md) for the tier-1 model card,
[Language](../domains/language.md) for the phonology and the content→render
seam behind every name and telling here, [Study 004, the Census of
Faiths](../laboratory/study-004.md) for the two rotation calibrations this
pair exemplifies at the scale of ten thousand worlds, [Study 007, the Census
of Eyes](../laboratory/study-007.md) for the species calibrations, [Study 008,
the Census of Tongues](../laboratory/study-008.md) for the naming
calibrations, [Campaign 5, The Gods](../chronicle/campaign-5.md) for the
Year-1 retrospective this capstone originally closed, [Firm Ground
II](../chronicle/campaign-y2-0.md) for the placement fix that gave this page
its sharper shape, [The Eyes](../chronicle/15-the-eyes.md) for the perception
vector that gave it a second pantheon, and [The
Tongues](../chronicle/16-the-tongues.md) for the voice that gave it its own
words.
