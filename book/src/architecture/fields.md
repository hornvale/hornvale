# Fields

A **field** is a typed function over space and time: ask it for a value at a
position and a moment, and it answers — the same answer every time, by
constitutional requirement. Elevation is a field. Temperature is a field.
So, eventually, are the stranger quantities the social simulation needs:
population pressure, scarcity, religious fervor, misery, trade intensity.

Fields are the **coarse** half of "coarse constrains fine." They describe the
world statistically and continuously, at any zoom, without naming anything.
A field can tell you *this valley has been under high raiding pressure for a
century*; it cannot tell you the raider chieftain's name. That division of
labor is deliberate — the named, discrete, causal half lives in the
[fact ledger](./ledger.md), and the [refinement engine](./refinement.md)
mediates between them.

Time enters as simulated days since world genesis — a plain number. There is
no wall-clock time anywhere in Hornvale; a field queried about day 4,000
answers identically whether asked today or in ten years.

As of Campaign 1a two field varieties exist: the constant field (the same
value everywhere, forever — tier-0 climate will be exactly this) and the
noise field (a seeded fractal noise surface with a feature wavelength — the
first ingredient of terrain). The interesting fields — derived fields that
compose others, and the social-historical fields — arrive with the domains
that need them.
