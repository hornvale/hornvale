# The Diachronic Book

*A Book that knows nothing on its first day, and by its hundredth year
has priests who can tell you when the sky will next go dark.*

This is the eighth and final campaign of the self-writing-book program,
and it gives the Book the one dimension it lacked: time. Every volume now
ends with **The Reckoning of Years**, two snapshots of the same world's
knowledge — its first days, and its hundredth year:

> **In the first days**
> The sky keeps no dates to number.
>
> **In the hundredth year**
> The sky has darkened, now and again.
> The priesthood of the Vavako numbers the darkenings: 4010.
> The next darkening, it teaches, comes on day 36526.
>
> *In truth, the darkenings of the first hundred years number 6472.*

Nothing new was drawn and nothing new was committed to any world.
Knowledge at time T is a pure derivation: the sky's eclipses are already
a closed-form ledger, and what a culture holds at T is what it could
have witnessed and kept — solar darkenings are public, lunar ones belong
to night-capable eyes, and *keeping* is the institution's gift. A folk
culture remembers qualitatively, forever: "the sky has darkened, now and
again." An organized priesthood keeps records, and records climb a
ladder — counted, then numbered, then **predictive**, once enough events
of one recurrence stand in the diaries. This is the Babylonian
astronomy of it: the temple's tablets becoming the saros becoming the
power to prophesy.

And the prophecy is checkable, and true. The predicted day is verified
against the world's own future — a standing law asserts that every
taught prediction names a real coming event. The cell the doctrine
campaign measured structurally empty — the claim folk could check —
fills diachronically: by the hundredth year the priesthood makes dated,
falsifiable claims, and is vindicated every time, which is how
institutional authority compounds when the institution's records are
genuinely better than memory.

The margins do the honest half, as they have since the chorus began.
Seed 1's priesthood numbers 4010 darkenings with perfect bookkeeping —
and the margin counts 6472, because two peoples with day-dwellers' eyes
never saw the 2,462 lunar eclipses their own sky held. Perfect records
of an imperfect vantage: the margin is the exact, derived difference
between what a knowledge-state holds and what the world did. And in the
world where a nocturnal people's priesthood once counted everything, the
close's own absorptions told the last story: a moisture epoch from a
parallel campaign reshaped that world's societies, its kobold cult went
folk, and its perfect count now stands in the Book as a folk memory —
still 81 events witnessed, no longer anyone to number them.

The explorer's lens ships beside the committed page: `hornvale book
--at <day>` renders any volume at any knowledge-state — day 20,000, day
50,000 — with counts grown and predictions re-dated, never committed,
always derivable. The vessel seam (the possessed session reading the
Book at its own day) is the recorded next step into the game layer.

The ceiling stays honestly above: knowledge that *decays*, margins that
reopen as arts are lost, the anomaly that breaks a paradigm, the schism
that forks one account into two lineages — all of it needs memory,
paradigm, and faction dynamics the world model doesn't yet have, and all
of it remains named on the registry rather than faked at the floor.

With this campaign the program's committed sequence — the first
sentence, the predicates and the grammar, the echo, the tongues, the
chorus, the explanations, the doctrine, the deep grammar, and now the
reckoning — is complete. The Book writes itself: from a fact graph to a
gazetteer that speaks every people's language, disagrees with itself
exactly where its cultures have earned the disagreement, teaches and
withholds, marks its words with how it knows them, and now grows wiser
on schedule. What stands beyond is the north star the metaplan named
and deliberately did not promise: the engine that writes the world's
book turning to write its own.
