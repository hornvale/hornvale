# The Branches

**July 2026 · outcome: complete, merged — gives the world its first
language family, and along the way discovers and fixes a standing defect
in how the world seats its peoples**

## What was attempted

The Words gave every people its own tongue and drew a bright line in
front of the next obvious question: goblin and kobold each aged a
proto-root through their own sound-change cascade, in isolation, so a
resemblance between two languages' words — had one ever appeared — would
have been coincidence, not kinship. Nothing in the world held a shared
ancestor two languages could actually be measured against. The Branches
steps over that line on purpose, in the smallest way that produces a real
**language family**: one authored ancestor, proto-goblinoid, and three
living daughters — goblin, hobgoblin, and bugbear — descending from it,
each through its own cascade, landing on its own present-day mouth. Kobold
stays exactly what it was: a family of one, sharing no ancestor with the
goblinoids, standing as the negative control a family needs.

Two new peoples joining the world was never going to be a change language
could absorb alone. Hobgoblin and bugbear needed bodies, minds, and a
place to stand, which meant this campaign also had to reckon with what
adding two more claimants to a single globe's best cells actually does to
the placement machinery every earlier campaign had only ever exercised
with two.

## What landed

**A shared ancestor, not a resemblance.** Proto-goblinoid is authored as a
language with no speakers: an ancestral point in the same six-dimension
articulation space every people's phonology already occupies, equal to no
daughter's own vector, so that "all three diverge" is a fact about the
family tree rather than a description of two lineages and a third that
never moved. One proto-root per concept is drawn from it, once, at the
family level, and shared unmodified by all three daughters. A daughter
still draws its own cascade and its own present phonology exactly as The
Words shipped — this campaign rewrites no rule — but now evolves the
*shared* root through that cascade rather than a root it drew for itself.
Three cascades over one root necessarily produce three related, regularly
correspondent modern forms: a true cognate set, provable rather than
merely asserted.

**Nativization: the mouth keeps what it can hold.** Evolving a shared
ancestor exposed a failure mode a same-species draw never had — an
inherited sound no rule in a daughter's cascade ever touches, and that
daughter's own phonology never happened to draw either. That segment is
now **nativized**: merged, by a fixed and draw-free function, to the
nearest segment the daughter's own inventory holds, measured by
articulatory-feature distance. `evolve` gained a stronger guarantee as a
result — a daughter's modern form is always a subset of its own
inventory — and a daughter's descent genuinely *loses* ancestral contrasts
the way a real language absorbs a sound its speakers no longer make,
rather than merely reshaping one. The three daughters' three different
inventories, each shaped by that species' own voice loudness exactly as
The Tongues first wired it, decide how much of proto-goblinoid's phonemic
space each one keeps distinct and how much it collapses — the family's
real, measured source of divergence.

**The family made visible.** The dictionary grows a Cognates section: for
every concept every goblinoid daughter still holds a root for, one row
showing the shared proto-form beside all three daughters' modern forms and
a plain descent gloss (`*gʷat → wat- / vad- / gwad-`), kobold excluded by
construction rather than by a filter, because a singleton family has
nothing to be a cognate *with*. A new reference page renders
proto-goblinoid itself — its inventory, its phonotactics, its full
proto-root table — the same way the phonology and dictionary pages already
render a living language's.

**A seed-swept battery, not a seed-42 story.** A dedicated study walks
1,000 seeds asking whether the family holds as a rule rather than a fact
about one world: every cascade is regular on every seed with zero
exceptions; every goblinoid root traces to the shared ancestor on every
seed with zero exceptions; every daughter's every modern form sits inside
that daughter's own inventory on every seed with zero exceptions. Two
findings came back measured rather than assumed, and both are reported
exactly as measured. The outgroup is clean on 998 of 1,000 seeds — kobold
shares no proto with any goblinoid — and the two exceptions are exactly
what a finite phoneme space predicts at that sample size: a short
proto-root landing on an identical segment sequence by pure chance, the
same birthday-paradox tail any comparative method has to live with, not a
leak in the family boundary. And the loudness-driven ordering of how much
each daughter's descent diverges — quietest daughter drifts furthest,
loudest drifts least — holds decisively at the population level (a
roughly 1.8x gap between the two ends) but not on every individual seed;
three independently noisy per-seed counts were never going to rank
identically a thousand times running, and the battery says so rather than
rounding the finding up to an invariant it isn't.

## The globe was never built for four

Two more peoples claiming a place to live is a small sentence that turned
out to hide a real defect. The settlement formula that had served two
peoples perfectly well scores every habitable cell against a species'
own psychology, and the first authored draft of hobgoblin's psychology
scored *better than goblin's on every single term at once* — a stronger
people that doesn't merely outcompete a weaker one for the best ground,
but Pareto-dominates it, leaving the weaker people nothing anywhere on the
map. Under that draft, goblin and bugbear placed zero settlements across
every seed tried: a family whose own baseline daughter had no settlements,
no names, and no cognates to show for it, which is to say a language
family that could not be observed.

Re-authoring hobgoblin's psychology to cede a real niche — a tighter,
less coastal-hungry legion, a bugbear that reads a threat harder before it
flees one — bought each of the four peoples a corner of the globe nothing
else wanted: goblin holds the coastal temperate belt it always has,
hobgoblin a watered frontier, kobold the inland freshwater interior,
bugbear the dry badlands. No pair Pareto-dominates another anymore. But a
niche only guarantees a people the *best* cell for it if nothing else
claims that cell first, and the placement algorithm's spacing rule — built
to keep two peoples' settlements from crowding the same ground — could
still box a weaker people out to a single, lonely founding settlement even
inside a niche it nominally owned uncontested.

The fix that shipped is a small, deterministic guarantee sitting ahead of
the ordinary competitive fill: before spacing runs at all, every people
reserves its own single best habitable cell, full stop, immune to any
other people's radius or rank. Every people gets a flagship no matter how
the rest of the map sorts itself out afterward; everything past that first
reservation still competes normally. It is the narrowest fix that unblocks
observability today, not a general answer to how four (or forty) peoples
ought to share one world — that wider question, what a settlement
algorithm optimizing one cell's suitability at a time is actually doing to
the shape of an entire ecosystem, is written up at length in
[the frontier's essay on how the weak
persist](../frontier/frontier.md#the-biogeography-of-coexistence--how-the-weak-persist),
which this campaign's own defect is what surfaced.

## What was learned

- **A shared ancestor is cheap; a shared globe is not.** Lifting a
  proto-root from one species to a family of three was, in the end, a
  small and clean change to one function's input. Seating two more bodies
  on the same map the placement formula had only ever balanced for two
  is where the real cost of this campaign turned out to live — a
  reminder that a language family's difficulty was never really about
  language.
- **A formula tuned for two guests does not automatically host four.**
  Nothing about the settlement-suitability weights was wrong for the
  world it was built in; they simply encoded no opinion at all about what
  happens when a third and fourth claimant show up and one of them turns
  out to be better at everything. The defect was invisible until a second
  pair of new peoples made it visible, which is the ordinary shape of a
  scaling problem, not a one-off.
- **Report the measurement, not the tidier story.** The clean-outgroup and
  divergence-ordering findings could each have been asserted as a clean
  invariant and nobody outside the Lab would have checked. Both are
  reported instead exactly as measured — two coincidental collisions kept
  in, a per-seed chain that holds barely more than half the time kept
  in — because a battery that only ever reports success is not a battery,
  it is decoration.

## Deferred, deliberately

Every concept a goblinoid daughter lexicalizes keeps the root it
inherited; no daughter innovates a new root or loses one it was given, so
the true rate at which real languages replace their vocabulary — the
Dolgopolsky ranking a comparative linguist would recognize — is not yet a
fact this world can show. No word crosses a family boundary either:
daughters do not borrow from one another, so loanword resistance and the
areal features a Sprachbund would produce wait on contact between peoples
who have actually met. And nothing here is *inferred* — the proto, the
cascades, and every cognate set are asserted from ground truth the sim
already holds, never reconstructed blind from surface wordlists the way a
real historical linguist has to work; a blind comparative method scored
against this campaign's own ground truth is the capstone the whole
descent arc has been building toward. A linguistic family this deliberate
also implies a species clade, and wiring the language tree to a deeper
species phylogeny is noted as a future consistency constraint, not built
as one.
