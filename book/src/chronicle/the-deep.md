# The Deep

**July 2026 · outcome: merged — the world gains an *under*: a coarse
stratigraphic column beneath every cell that is at once the vertical dimension
terrain lacked and the deep-time archive the frozen earth withheld — built
without moving a single existing byte**

## What was attempted

Crust answered *how high* the land stands and The Ground answered *what it is
made of*, but both describe a surface. The world had no *under*: no depth
coordinate, no sense that the rock beneath a meadow is younger than the rock
beneath it, no venue for the lightless, the sealed, the forgotten. And [Deep
Time](./deep-time.md) had deliberately frozen the solid earth — varying only
ice and sea level over fixed relief — which left the one place the past is
most legible unread. Stone *is* a record: layer on layer, the deepest the
oldest. This campaign is the first stone of that reading, and it takes the
underworld in the project's usual order, coarse before fine.

The organizing idea is that **depth is a single axis along which several things
vary at once**: downward is older (deeper strata record earlier ages), and
older is more forgotten (the archive has gaps where time left no trace). Space
and time are therefore not two systems here — the column beneath a cell simply
*is* its history, read top-down.

## What shipped

A per-cell **stratigraphic column**, computed as a pure projection over fields
the tectonic globe already holds — crust age and thickness, the lithology
buffer, sediment load, the crystalline basement — so it draws no new randomness
and commits no new fact. Five bands, coarsening downward: the regolith skin,
the sedimentary cover where the archive is most legible, the basement inherited
unchanged from the globe, the hot deep **Roots**, and the primordial
**Underneath** — the threshold to what lies below. Each band is stamped with
the era it records; where thin cover sits directly on ancient basement, the
column reports an **unconformity** — missing time, the geological echo of a
memory let slip.

Alongside it, a **geothermal field**: the gradient of warmth with depth, hot
under young thin crust and cool under ancient shields. It is framed as the
deep's *energy base* — the sunless world's equivalent of insolation — so the
subterranean ecology a later campaign will grow has something to feed on.

The column surfaces three ways: a **depth-to-basement map lens** (pale where
bedrock lies near the surface, dark over the deep sedimentary basins); three
new **census metrics** (mean depth to basement, unconformity fraction, mean
geothermal gradient); and an almanac **"The Deep"** section that reads the
archive back in prose — how far to basement, the span of the deep's warmth, how
much of the land forgot an age, and, where a glacial past exists, the ice's
mark left in the cover.

Because every output is derived and nothing is drawn or committed, the campaign
is **not an epoch**: the seed-42 world serializes byte-for-byte as it did
before, and the world-identity fixture never moved.

## What it opens, and what it deliberately left

The Deep is the inert first rung of a longer ascent. It builds the substrate
and reserves the hooks; it does not build the agents. Deferred, in order:
located features and the dimensional gates that pierce the ordered column;
the fine three-dimensional graph and the lightless venue vocabulary a
descending people will need; the subterranean ecology; the surface–under
relationship in full; and the whole magical layer — a second, thaumic energy
and the numen that rises with depth — which stays firmly behind the metaphysics
gate for its own campaign. This campaign is purely geological by design: the
reserved thaumic axis remains exactly zero, the honest floor a later, bolder
tier will refine and never contradict.
