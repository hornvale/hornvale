# The Temperament

A creature in Hornvale could already want one thing. [The Wanting](./the-wanting.md)
gave it thirst — a single homeostatic drive, a setpoint that rose over days and
was reset by a drink, and a reactive controller that walked it to water. But a
mind with one want is not yet a mind. The moment a second need appears, the
question stops being *how do I get water* and becomes *water or warmth, which
matters more, right now* — and that question, arbitration, is where temperament
lives. This campaign closed the first edge of cognition by giving creatures a
second drive, a way to resolve the two that their psychology shapes, a felt
state legible to a reader, and a way for the population to score its own health.

The second drive is thermal comfort, and it is a different *kind* of drive than
thirst. Thirst is a stock: urgency accrues while you are away from water and
discharges in a discrete drink. Comfort is a flow: a creature reads the
temperature of the very cell it stands in against its species' niche, and the
discomfort is instantaneous — no memory, no plan, just a step toward a kinder
neighbour. Both, though, present to the arbiter as the same shape — an urgency
and an affordance — so a single mechanism resolves them without knowing which is
which. It scores every candidate action by the weighted sum of the drives it
serves, capped so survival always outranks comfort (a creature dying of thirst
ignores the cold; a merely thirsty one still seeks warmth), and commits to an
errand with hysteresis so it does not dither at the boundary between two
near-equal needs.

Psychology enters as a single dial. A creature's *deliberation latency* slides
that weighting between two poles: *grab*, where only the loudest drive counts —
the impulsive mind, serving whatever screams — and *weigh*, the full weighted
sum — the deliberate mind, trading one need against another. The same two
drives in the same cell resolve to different actions depending on where a
species sits on that dial. Temperament is not a table of rules; it is one
number bending one arbitration.

From that arbitration a creature's *affect* falls out for free — a pure read of
the decision, never stored, matching the campaign's founding discipline that a
drive is a fold and nothing more. It is a point in the psychological circumplex,
valence against arousal: content when nothing presses; eager when a need is met
or a known source is in reach; searching when following a gradient toward an
unknown one; frustrated when a known source is out of reach; lost when there is
no basis to move at all. The distinction between *searching* and *lost* is
load-bearing — a creature seeking not-yet-found water is puttering normally, and
counting that as distress would make everything downstream meaningless. Affect
carries what it is *about* — frustrated *about water* — so a reader of a
possessed world sees not just that a creature frets but what for, and the felt
state renders as diegetic prose that tracks the drive across its cycle.

The last two pieces turn that felt state into an instrument and a payoff. The
population's affect, simulated forward over many days and reduced across its
creatures, is a **health metric** — the epidemiology of a world's minds:
prevalence of distress now, chronicity of the persistently stuck, the half-life
of a spike that recovers, decomposed by cause and by species. It is anchored to
a control, and the control taught the campaign something. A healthy world reads
*zero* — condensed, on-water settlements let creatures drink in place, and a
creature denied water *searches* rather than despairs, so genuine
blocked-distress almost never arises on its own. The metric's value is therefore
not a number that varies but a number that stays quiet: it is a regression
alarm, armed precisely by reading zero on every healthy world, that would fire
the moment the cognition layer broke in a way that left a mind stuck. And with
creatures now reading cold and heat against their niche and acting on the
discomfort, those two concepts finally earn their cognitive handle — the
correspondence audit's cognition column moves from nothing covered to its first
two edges, the wave that this campaign began.
