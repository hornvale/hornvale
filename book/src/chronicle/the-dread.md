# The Dread

Creatures in Hornvale thirsted, shivered, tired, slept, and hungered — but
nothing frightened them. They would walk into a cursed grove or a lethal waste
as blithely as a meadow. A world observed through text needs its creatures to
*flinch*: to shun the uncanny, to route around a hazard on the way to water, to
recoil where a place is wrong. This campaign added the fifth drive, danger, and
gave them fear.

The result that shaped the design is a symmetry. Danger is the **avoidance twin
of hunger** — the same machinery run in reverse. Hunger's food-value is a
creature's diet niche dotted with a cell's resources, and a hungry creature
*climbs* that field. Danger's threat-value is a threat niche dotted with a cell's
hazards, and a frightened creature *flees* it. Approach and avoidance are one
shape reflected: `threat_niche · hazard_field` is `diet_niche · resource_field`
with the sign turned over. So danger reuses the flow-drive template thermal comfort
established — sensing the field at the cell it occupies, stepping down the
gradient rather than up — and, like thermal, it carries no internal stock and no
discharge event. Fear is not *reset* the way thirst is reset by a drink; it
simply lifts when the threat is gone. It is the leanest drive yet: no new
committed fact, no new action. Fleeing is an ordinary step toward the safest
neighbour.

What makes danger more than another competitor is a reading borrowed from robotics
and traffic engineering, where navigation is an attractive goal-field summed with
*repulsive obstacle fields*. Danger is the first drive whose serviceability is
**signed**: a step toward safety is served, but a step *into* worse danger scores
negative. Because the mind weighs a single sum of utility over all its active
drives, a move that would slake thirst but cross a hazard nets thirst's pull minus
danger's penalty — so a thirsty creature routes *around* the deadly ground rather
than straight through it. Danger does not merely compete with the other drives; it
reshapes the paths they take. And where every step leads into dread with nowhere
safer, the creature holds, cornered, and reads the high-arousal fear the
circumplex has always plotted top-left.

One collision moved the design, as they always do. A flow drive senses only the
cell it stands on — but a creature standing safely *beside* a hazard would then
feel nothing, and could not refuse the step into it. Fear, though, is
anticipatory: you dread the dangerous ground *ahead*, not only the ground
underfoot. So danger senses the greatest threat across its cell and the cells one
step away, and is roused while a hazard is merely adjacent — which is exactly what
lets its signed serviceability veto the step before it is taken.

The threat itself, in a world with no predators yet, is the **uncanny**. A
timeline of the drive's own evolution — reflexive startle, then the bronze-age
taboo of the cursed place, then calculated risk, then the game theory of predator
and prey — places the honest coarse rung at the taboo: instinctive avoidance of
*strange ground*. Every room already carries a strangeness magnitude, the mark of
the exotic sites scattered across the world, and a creature now shuns them,
raised to lethal where the very climate would kill. When active predation ships,
a predator agent becomes simply a moving source in this same field; danger, the
perception-side of the hunt, is built first. Reserved with it, correct by
construction the moment such a creature walks: the per-kind **threat niche**,
where a weight may go *negative* — fire that threatens the warm-blooded is *home*
to a fire elemental, holy ground that is safe to the living is dread to the
undead — and a **boldness** dial whose far extreme turns fear into its opposite,
the moth to the flame, the hunter toward its prey.

The change reached only where it should. Danger draws no seed, stores no state,
commits no fact — genesis is byte-identical, and so, this time, is the possessed
world: seed 42's herder never wanders near a strange site, so its fear stays
correctly silent and its transcript unchanged. The dread waits in the field, for
the creatures whose paths cross the uncanny, and for the predators still to come.
