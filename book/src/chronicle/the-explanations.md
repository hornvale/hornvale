# The Explanations

*Two peoples watch one sunrise, and give two reasons.*

The chorus, as the fourth campaign left it, could say what each culture
holds. This campaign — the metaplan's fifth — makes each culture say
**why**. Seed 1's volume now reads:

> **As the Vavako tell it.**
> Vebe is the earth.
> The day returns because the sky must be crossed.
>
> **As the Babako tell it.**
> Vebe is the earth.
> The day returns, as all things return.

The goblins hold a path cosmology: the sky is a road, and roads demand
crossing. The hobgoblins hold a cycle cosmology: return is simply the
nature of things, and needs no traveler. Elsewhere in the roster the sky
gets an agent — a seed-2 kobold's *"Nggo strides the sky, slowly"*, a
seed-10 hobgoblin's *"Kaa stalks the sky, briskly"* — and at two measured
seeds the kobolds explain the moon they alone can count as kin: *"The
moon crosses because it is Qaazhoo's kin."* Same physics under every one
of these sentences; different worldviews on top; and nothing anywhere
authored per culture.

The machinery is the causal filter — the fifth and last of the epistemic
stack's derivational filters, the one the fourth campaign deferred. What
is authored is authored once, globally, and closed: a library of twelve
embodied causal schemas (force, agency, flow, container, path, balance,
sympathy, kinship, debt, cycle, essence, verticality — the source-domains
of conceptual metaphor, which is why the list is bounded rather than
open), a small table of verbs gated by how a people lives, and six
sentence frames. Everything per-culture is derived or drawn: schema
priors from subsistence and society (herders lean kinship, foragers lean
agency), an explanatory-monomania dial from how the culture assigns
standing, the explaining deity from the culture's own generated pantheon,
and the final choice by seeded draw — because deriving a people's
cosmology *wholly* from its psychology would ship astrology as science,
and the draw is where contingency honestly lives.

Two constraints did the campaign's real shaping. The first is anonymity:
no consumer of the trace protocol may know which system produced a
phenomenon, so the engine cannot ask for "the sun god." Instead it binds
by rhythm — the deity whose worshipped period matches the day the culture
lives — and manner the same way: the sun-deity walks *briskly* not
because a standard-day number leaked into a goblin's mouth, but because
its period is the shortest among the culture's own cyclic gods. The
second is invisibility to the instruments: the distinctiveness ×
recoverability dial the fourth campaign put in the census reads
explanations not at all — an explained fact measures exactly as its
underlying knowledge-state, proven by equality tests, by mutation, and by
the fifty-seed study fixture coming back byte-identical. Explanation
changes what a culture *says*, never what the ledger holds or what the
census measures; the margin still carries the true number under every
folk cosmology.

The honest edges are named where they live. Every explanation sentence
parses back and re-realizes byte-identically (the corpus law holds for
"because" as it holds for everything else). A culture whose pantheon
offers no matching rhythm explains nothing — the entry stays a plain
loss, because a synthetic agent would be authored worldview. And the
standing laws that watch for vacuity fail loudly in both directions: if
every culture ever converges on one schema, or one verb, the suite does
not shrug — it demands the collapse be re-derived deliberately. At
today's floor the competition is real: three distinct day-cosmologies
across the first three seeds, drawn from priors every culture shares.

What waits is deliberate. Doctrine and institutions — who *teaches* the
explanation — are the sixth campaign's. The tongues themselves rendering
the because-clause, with evidential morphology marking how the speaker
knows, are the seventh's. And the loop where a live metaphor dies into
the lexicon — where "the sky must be crossed" fossilizes into a word for
west — belongs to the etymology engine, on the far side of the
diachronic Book.
