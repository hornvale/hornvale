# The Lode

**July 2026 · outcome: merged — the underworld gains its first *contents*:
located, typed caves and ore deposits, derived over The Deep's column, without
moving a committed byte**

## What was attempted

The Deep gave the world an *under* — a stratigraphic column and its deep-time
archive — but the column was empty of features: no caves to descend, no ore to
seek. The Ground had shipped `cave_proneness` and `prospectivity` as
*probability fields*, and named their promotion to actual bodies as future
work. The Lode is that promotion: the world's first **caves** (where the rock
holds a void) and **ore deposits** (where fluids concentrated a metal), each a
located, typed feature living in the column — the next rung up the underworld's
autonomy ladder, from an inert record to emplaced-but-inert anomalies.

The organizing insight is that a cave and an ore vein are **two faces of one
subsurface fluid flow**: dissolution opens the void, precipitation fills the
vein. Carbonate country dissolves into karst caves *and* hosts lead-zinc; fault
zones open fracture caves *and* channel hydrothermal gold; basalt provinces
drain into lava-tubes *and* carry copper. So caves and deposits are not derived
independently — both ride one shared fluid-flow gate, and cave country and ore
country **co-locate by construction**. The delver who follows the caves finds
the veins.

## What shipped

A deterministic **point process** over the fields terrain already owns: a
hash-noise gate, biased along tectonic lineaments so ore forms **belts** rather
than uniform scatter, decides where a feature sits. Caves carry a **type**
(karst, lava-tube, or fracture, read from lithology) and a depth-reach into the
column. Ore deposits are organized by **genetic process** — magmatic copper on
subduction arcs, hydrothermal gold in fault veins, carbonate-hosted lead-zinc,
banded iron on ancient cratons, pegmatite gems, alluvial placers, and
salt/coal projected directly from the rock beds that *are* the ore. Each
deposit carries a commodity, a column depth, a **grade** drawn from a
many-poor-few-rich distribution (so a deposit's richness is unknown until it is
found), and a tonnage that sets whether it is a lone delver's rich vein or an
industrial low-grade body. One deposit is **climate-coupled** — laterite
(bauxite) forms only where the weathering is hot and wet — and is wired at the
composition root exactly as soil is.

The features surface as a map lens (`--field features`), an almanac **"The
Lode"** section (dominant commodities, cave country, co-located regions), and
census metrics (cave fraction, deposit density, dominant commodity, mean
grade). Every output is a **pure derived query**: no new randomness beyond
reused hash-noise, no committed fact, no changed byte — the seed-42 world
serializes exactly as it did, and world identity never moved.

## What it leaves reserved

The Lode is deliberately the *derived, inert* half of the features rung, and
purely geological. The **event-emplaced** half — mines and delvings, sealed
vaults, dimensional gates, and the mechanism by which a seal fails when the
civilization that maintained it declines — waits for its own campaign, where
the underworld first gains latent agency. **Magical ores** (the legendary tier
above gold and gems) stay behind the metaphysics gate, the rarity spectrum's
top rung left open for them. And *knowing* where the ore is — the prospector's
craft, a people's lore of good delving stone — is a later, cultural layer; the
deposit ships here as neutral truth, waiting to be found.
