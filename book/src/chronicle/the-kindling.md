# The Kindling

[The Temperament](./the-temperament.md) gave creatures the first edge of
cognition — two drives, a psychology that arbitrates between them, a felt state,
and a metric by which a population scores its own health. Three followups
sharpened that edge without a chronicle of their own: a synthetic distress
harness that drives the real sim into despair so the health metric's alarms are
proven end to end rather than on hand-typed traces; a second psychology dial,
`time_horizon`, so a foresighted creature pre-empts a need before it bites; and
learned helplessness, so a creature whose survival need goes unmet long enough
stops trying, its felt state deepening from *frustrated* to *given up*.

But under all of it, every creature still burned the same. Thirst was universal
— every derived agent carried it — and it rose at a flat rate regardless of the
world around it, so a creature crossing a desert dehydrated no faster than one
resting in a temperate glade, and an undead that has no metabolism to run dry
thirsted like anything alive. The drive layer had simply never asked what *kind*
of body it was animating. The species domain, it turned out, had been answering
that question all along: every species carries a `MetabolicClass` —
warm-blooded, cold-blooded, phototroph, or ametabolic — and the cold-blooded
case even carried a note, parked since the species campaign, that its rate
should couple to ambient temperature. The model existed; it had never reached
the drives. This campaign carried it the last step.

The first move is a gate. An ametabolic creature — a construct, an undead, an
elemental — has no homeostatic drives at all: it neither thirsts nor
thermoregulates. With no need to close, it reads *content* forever, an eerie
stillness that is exactly right for the deathless. Today this changes nothing
underfoot — the four peoples who build settlements are all warm- or
cold-blooded, and the ametabolic kinds are the mighty who never settle — so the
gate is latent but correct, firing the moment a bodiless thing becomes an agent.
It is also the seam on which those creatures' *own* motivations will later hang:
a construct's integrity, an undead's hunger for the living, an elemental fed by
the very element that would parch anything else.

The second move couples the living's thirst to the world's heat. A metabolizing
creature's thirst is no longer a flat rate times elapsed time; it is a path
integral of a *dehydration rate* over the creature's actual occupancy since its
last drink — for every stretch it stood in one cell, the rate that cell's
temperature commands, times the time it spent there. The rate depends on the
body: an endotherm sweats, so heat above its thermoneutral zone accelerates the
loss while cold leaves it at base; an ectotherm's whole realized rate tracks the
climate, fast in the heat and torpid in the cold, floored so a frozen one still
needs some water. From this a small ecological signature falls out for free —
cold-blooded peoples are disadvantaged in deserts and advantaged in the cold,
never authored, only implied by the coupling.

The determinism discipline is the quiet achievement. Thirst is a derived view,
never stored, so both the tick that moves a creature and the stateless read that
reports its feeling must compute the same integral from the same committed
history, or the creature would act on one thirst and be narrated with another.
They share a single fold, re-derived over the committed occupancy each time, so
they agree by construction rather than by careful alignment; and at a
thermoneutral or unreadable climate the integral collapses back to the old flat
rate exactly, so every world that was byte-identical before remains so. What is
realized here is the behavioral, water-facing half of the species campaign's
deferred coupling; the energetic, allometric half — and the phototrophs'
transpiration, and the ametabolic kinds' own drives — remain kindling for a
later fire.
