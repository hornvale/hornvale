# The Wilding

The Quarry built a fear and left it sleeping. It wove a field of predator presence
across the world and derived, from each creature's own diet, how much it should
dread being eaten — and then it noted that the peoples who walk the world today are
bold and omnivorous, and shrug that dread off. The fear was wired, correct, and
inert. It waited, the chronicle said, "for the day a vulnerable creature opens its
eyes as an agent." This campaign opens those eyes.

Until now the wild was a *statistic*. The carnivores that thin the coexistence stack,
the herbivores that graze the productive cells — all of them existed only as
densities, numbers over the map, a field the peoples' drives could read but which
had no drives of its own. A world observed through text was peopled solely by its
*peoples*: the derived NPCs a possession session raised were the goblins and kobolds
of the settlements, and the beasts were scenery. The Wilding makes the wild into
individuals. From the same demography stack The Quarry fit its predator field to,
`worldgen::wild_concentrations` reads the world's **beast** concentrations — the
mobile, non-autotrophic species, ranked by their realized mass, each pinned to the
cell where it is densest: a herd here, a lair there. `vessel::derive_wild_npcs` mints
those concentrations as agents and appends them to the peopled roster, in the
possession session and in the population-health simulation alike. Where the world
had five kinds of derived mind, it now has the fauna too: a wild rust-monster, a wild
otyugh, a wild xorn, a wild giant-elk.

A beast is not a person, and the derivation honors the difference. A wild species is,
by construction, one absent from the psyche registry — it has no authored
temperament — so every wild agent takes the steady, inert default of boldness and
deliberation the peoples earn by authorship. But its *nature* it keeps in full: its
threat niche is derived from its biosphere exactly as a person's is, so the
giant-elk and the woolly-mammoth — herbivores, prey — carry the full weight of
predator-dread, while the rust-monster, a carnivore, carries almost none. And so the
thing The Quarry built comes true at last. A herbivore beast, placed on the ground
where carnivores range, now feels it for what it is and gives it a wide berth —
predator-fear as a *live* flight, not a sleeping potential. The meek have opened
their eyes.

Nothing of the world itself changed. The wild agents are derived, like the drives
themselves, from facts already committed at genesis — no new seed draw, no new
predicate, no epoch — so every world is byte-for-byte what it was. What changed is
the *observation*: possess a world now and its fauna walks alongside its peoples,
the herd stirring across the valley while the settlement's own folk drink in place.
The possession galleries record it plainly — where the day-long wait once sensed a
handful of movements, it now senses a thousand, the wild going about its business.
The population-health metric, too, now takes the pulse of the fauna: a world's
creatures fear their predators, and a natural world — one with somewhere to flee —
carries that fear without ever falling into chronic distress. The alarm stays
silent; the wild is well.

The wild that hunts is still to come. These beasts fear predators, but no beast yet
*is* a hunter — the visible predator that stalks and eats, the GOAP-planned pursuit,
the slow Lotka-Volterra tides of predator and prey — all of it remains the reserved
body of the trophic engine. This campaign gave the wild eyes and fear. The teeth
come after.
