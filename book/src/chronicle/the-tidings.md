# The Tidings

The six Temperament drives had taught a creature to feel, and The Surmise had
taught it to plan over *belief* rather than truth — to walk toward the water it
remembered, not the water that is. But each creature remembered alone. One that
had never stood at a spring was ignorant of it forever unless it stumbled there;
one stranded far from the water it knew stayed stranded, though a neighbour at
its shoulder might know a nearer source. Nothing let one mind's knowledge relieve
another's. This campaign asked the first social-cognition question: can a band
circulate what it knows, so that the group, and not only the individual, becomes
the unit that corrects error?

The mechanism is a small, pure law. When a creature stands alone it consults its
own memory unchanged — the nearest water it has itself seen, ranked by distance
from home. But when it shares a locale with others, the band pools what its
members know and the creature reaches for whatever is nearest *to where it now
stands*. The split is deliberate, and it is what keeps the law honest about
determinism: a creature alone is a perfect no-op, so the live world — where the
derived folk are scattered one to a settlement and never share a room — is
byte-for-byte unchanged, its every almanac and transcript identical. Sharing is a
capability that lies dormant until true bands exist, and reconciles beliefs the
only way a deterministic world may: as a commutative fold over who stands where,
never a roll of dice.

The reach for water *here* rather than *home* was not the first design. It was
forced by the campaign's real discovery, which arrived — as the good ones do — in
the shape of a demonstration that refused to work. A creature is only ever
*distressed* by thirst when it knows of water it cannot reach; an ignorant one
merely searches, and searching is not suffering. So to show a band healing a
distressed member, one must strand a creature from a water it remembers and let a
neighbour point it to a nearer one. But belief is a fold recomputed every tick,
anchored to home — and the moment the rescued creature takes a step toward the
shared water, it leaves its neighbour's side, the fold runs again with no-one to
pool from, and its belief snaps back to the unreachable home water it started
with. The relief evaporates on the first stride. Stable rescue proved possible
only in a degenerate corner: a stationary informant standing on the very water
the sufferer already occupies, curing a distress that was itself an artifact of a
creature failing to recognise the puddle at its feet.

That corner is where the honest result lies, and it is worth more than a tidier
demonstration would have been. The sharing *law* is real, deterministic, and
wired into both the acting and the feeling of a creature; a co-located pair does
reconcile belief, and the health metric does register the relief. But the
band-as-error-correcting-unit — a knowledgeable member guiding a lost one across
the map to water it had never seen — cannot be built on belief that lives for a
single tick. It needs belief that *persists past the telling*: a creature must
carry what it was told away from the teller, the way it already carries a rumour
heard in a sentence but not yet what a neighbour knows of the world. That
persistence, hearsay that outlives its co-location, is the layer this campaign
proved necessary and left for the next. The tidings can be spoken; for now they
are forgotten the moment the speaker is out of sight. To make a band remember is
the work that follows.
