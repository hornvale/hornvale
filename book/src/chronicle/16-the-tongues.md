# Campaign 16: The Tongues

**July 2026 · 14 commits · outcome: complete, merged — planned and built
under the working name Y2-3, gives the world's two peoples their own
voices**

## What was attempted

Campaign 14 gave the world a second people and Campaign 15 gave each of them
its own eyes, but both still spoke with one borrowed mouth. Every proper
noun the world minted — a village, a god, a god's favorite title — came from
the same place: a short pool of syllables, drawn and capitalized, no
anatomy behind it, and a deity's epithet was picked from one of two fixed
English word lists regardless of which species was doing the venerating. A
kobold's caste ladder already read "digger" where a goblin's read "farmer,"
its gods already answered to a different lens and a different hour, but its
names sounded exactly like a goblin's, because nothing had ever asked what
a kobold's throat could actually produce. The Tongues set out to give both
peoples a real phonology — sounds an anatomy affords, not sounds an author
happened to seed — and to prove the difference by making a village's name,
a god's name, and the very shape of a myth's telling all trace back to
which mouth is speaking.

## What landed

**A sound is a bundle of features, never a string.** The substrate's
internal truth is never text. A phoneme is an authored point in a small
feature space — for a consonant, its place, manner, and voicing; for a
vowel, its height, backness, and rounding — the same move the world's minds
and eyes were already built from. Nothing about a sound is ever stored as a
spelling: it renders two ways, on demand, from the same feature bundle, a
plain-script surface form for the almanac and an IPA transcription for
anyone asking how it is actually said. A settlement's name and a god's
epithet inherit both renderings for free, and the two can never drift apart
from each other, because there was only ever one fact to begin with.

**A third closed vector, sitting beside the two that came before it.**
What a people's mouth can produce is authored exactly the way what a
people's mind wants and what a people's eyes notice already were: six
named capacities — how labial the mouth is, how wide its vowel space runs,
how sharply it marks voicing, how much it hisses, how loud it is willing to
be, and one signature exotic manner its anatomy affords — with goblin
sitting at every dimension's exact midpoint, so a goblin-only world's
generated names trace, by construction, the same formulas a kobold's do,
evaluated at the values that always meant "goblin." Kobold's column is read
off the same corpus its mind and its eyes were: a small, sibilant-heavy,
soft-voiced mouth built for a warren rather than an open field. The
envelope only says what a mouth can produce; the actual sounds a people
uses are drawn once per people, under that envelope's constraint, and
reused for every name that people ever generates afterward — same seed,
same language, every time.

**Names are grammar, not a pool.** A settlement's name, a deity's name, and
a deity's epithet all start from the same kind of object, a stem built from
drawn syllable templates over a drawn inventory, then shaped by a small set
of operations — joining two stems, doubling a syllable, affixing a bound
honorific drawn from its own class. Which names actually carry that
honorific is not left to chance: it keys to the same axis that already
decides whether a settlement's ladder tops out at a chief or at elders. A
people that marks status by rank affixes a dominance honorifics onto its
gods' titles; a people that marks it by knowledge or generosity builds
descriptive compounds instead, with no dominance marking anywhere in the
title. A god's epithet-shape recounts to how its people organizes
authority, exactly as legibly as its caste ladder already did.

**Meaning committed, voice rendered — a seam meant to outlive this
campaign.** Before this campaign, a belief committed a finished English
sentence and froze it into the record at the moment it was minted. That
stops here. A belief now commits *meaning*: a deity's generated name and
epithet, the phenomenon it mythologizes, whether that phenomenon is
watched, mourned-and-feasted, or felt through the ambient world rather than
seen, and its rank among its peers. A single function, living with the
tongues themselves and owned by no other domain, turns that meaning into
voiced text on demand, under three knobs derived from the same mind that
already shapes a people's caste ladder: how formal the telling is, how
often it repeats a refrain, and how many honorifics stack into one line. A
rank-basis people's myths read formal and honorific-dense; a knowledge- or
generosity-basis people's read repetitive and descriptive. Turning finished
English back into finished English by rewriting it after the fact was
considered and refused — a frozen sentence has nowhere to grow, but an
interface does, and a future teller with a richer grammar can occupy this
exact seam without touching the belief, the composition root, or the page
that reads the result.

**Two peoples, two unmistakably different pantheons, on one seed.** Seed
42's spinning sky crowns a goblin pantheon headed by **Nodvnotngak the
Nebsxad**, whose telling is formal and marks its own rank once, quietly,
in the record rather than in the words: *"Nodvnotngak the Nebsxad returns
every 0.88 days. So it was, so it is."* Beside it, in the warren its own
lens and hour raised, the kobold pantheon is headed by **Rragratxok
Ragxoq**, whose telling repeats its refrain and says its own rank aloud:
*"Rragratxok Ragxoq comes back every 15.99 days. That's how it's always
been. That's how it's always been. Rragratxok is the highest among the
gods."* Nothing about the two peoples' minds or eyes changed to produce
that contrast — only their mouths did, and the difference is legible in
every line either one speaks. Pin the same seed's sky to tidally locked and
the goblin head becomes an eternal watcher, **Xngeknet the
Sxodsxodsxodngod**, while the kobold pantheon stays lunar and unranked, a
folk cult led by **Rroqrroq Xoq** — the same divergence the sky and the
species axes were already proven to produce, now heard rather than only
read.

**A name space vast enough that collision becomes the exception, not the
rule.** A ten-thousand-world census measured what this campaign built
rather than assuming it: every generated name obeys its own species'
sound rules exactly, with no exceptions across the sample; every goblin
deity epithet carries its honorific and no kobold epithet ever does, a
structural fact read back from the committed sound itself rather than
echoed from the choice that produced it; and, weighed against the roughly
eleven-hundred-name ceiling the old fixed syllable pool lived under, the
generated name space collides in only 2.79% of worlds on average, with
nearly two-thirds of censused worlds carrying not one repeated name at all.
The old pool made a repeated name something to expect; the new one makes it
something worth noticing when it happens.

## What was learned

- **A closed vector earns its keep a third time.** Psychology, perception,
  and now articulation are each a small, named, authored space rather than
  a growing bag of traits — and the discipline holds exactly because every
  new dimension has to be argued for on its own, at the moment a mouth, an
  eye, or a mind is shown to need it, not folded in quietly because a
  struct happened to have room.
- **An interface, not a rewritten sentence, is the reusable part.** The
  campaign could have made every myth sound more varied by post-processing
  the same frozen English differently per species. It refused that path on
  purpose: a sentence generated once and touched up afterward has nowhere
  further to go, while a seam that takes structured meaning and renders it
  under a species' own voice can be occupied by a richer teller later
  without anyone upstream noticing the difference.
- **A vast name space is worth measuring, not merely trusting.** Nothing
  about the naming grammar promises uniqueness; it promises a large enough
  space that collision becomes rare. The census turned that promise into a
  number — 2.79% — rather than leaving it as an assumption nobody had
  checked.

## Deferred, deliberately

A lexicon and a syntax, and the meaning a name could carry once those
exist — today a name has shape and voice and nothing more, no gloss, no
etymology, nothing a listener could look up; sound-change, etymology, and
names as fossils of an older tongue, which want deep time under them before
they mean anything; meaningful toponyms, waiting on terrain features worth
naming; a people's everyday words for its own castes and trades, which
still borrow English because a phonology gives a name a sound, not a common
noun a meaning; pidgin, creole, and language-shift dynamics, which need two
peoples who have actually met; the richer, generative oral-formulaic
teller that the content→render seam was built to make room for without
anyone having to touch it again; and a paralinguistic layer, for the calls
and cries that are a different kind of sound than speech entirely.
