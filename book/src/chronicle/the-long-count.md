# The Long Count

**July 2026 · outcome: merged — the sky learns to work as a long clock: the
star ages on a derived slope, the solstice sunrise drifts with the obliquity
wobble and can date the sightlines cut against it, and the star, anchor, and
neighbors finally get the verification batteries the moons always had**

## What was attempted

A 2026-07-08 audit of the astronomy domain left four residuals on otherwise
shipped registry rows: eclipses recurred at a *rate* but never a *date*; the star's luminosity was frozen at its genesis value forever; the machinery that lets fixed stars drift with precession had no
ground-level counterpart — nothing connected the wobbling sky to a sightline
a builder might cut at a founding; and the domain's
verification surface was thinner than its billing, with moons enjoying
property batteries the star, anchor, and neighborhood never got.
This campaign set out to close all four.

## The collision, and what the campaign became

Thirteen minutes after this campaign's spec landed on main, a parallel
session committed *Eclipse Seasons* — a spec claiming the same new stream
(`moon-nodes`), the same derived nodal regression, and the same dated
solar-and-lunar event core, and going deeper on all of it: a positional moon
ephemeris, shadow ground tracks, the saros recurrence ladder. Two campaigns
implementing one stream label with independent draw semantics would have
been a save-format collision of the first order. The question went to Nathan
the hour it was found, and the adjudication was clean: Eclipse Seasons owns
the eclipse core; The Long Count re-scoped to the non-overlapping residuals
and hands the standstill cycle to a follow-up riding Eclipse Seasons'
deliberately deferred seam. The re-scoped campaign draws **nothing** — no
new stream, no epoch, not one moved byte in any existing draw — which the
golden-seed battery verified at every commit.

## The slow fire

Main-sequence stars brighten as they age — roughly ten percent per gigayear
at one solar mass, faster for heavier stars — and the domain now derives
that slope from the mass it already holds: `b = 0.10·M^2.5` per gigayear,
Sol-calibrated, scaled by the main-sequence lifetime. The derivation is
anchored so the present pays nothing: `luminosity_at(t = 0)` equals the
genesis draw exactly, and on the kiloyear scales the simulation inhabits the
slope is honestly negligible — it exists so deep time has the *right*
gradient to read, not to move today's sky. The residual's other half, semi-major-axis migration, was **declined with a decision record**
(0054): disk-driven migration ends before a stable habitable world exists,
and inventing post-genesis drift would have been exactly the
guess-wearing-physics'-clothes failure the domain's charter forbids. The
almanac now closes its Deep Time section with the sentence the campaign was
named for: the sun brightens by so many parts in a hundred over a gigayear —
the slow fire under every deeper clock.

## The sky as archaeological clock

Firm Ground II made the obliquity a slow function of time; the night-sky
instrument made the fixed stars drift with precession. What was missing was
the ground: where on the horizon the midsummer sun rises, and how that point
creeps as the obliquity wobbles. The calendar now answers three questions it
could not: the solstice-sunrise azimuth at any latitude and epoch
(`cos az = sin ε(t)/cos φ`, refraction declared away); the drift of that
azimuth between two epochs, which *is* the obliquity wobble seen from the
ground; and the inverse — given a sightline cut long ago and now measurably
stale, the most recent epoch that would have cut it. An unmoving sky
(the zero-forcing pin) honestly refuses to be dated. At genesis the
composition root commits each settlement's founding azimuth as a fact, so
the world's future archaeologists inherit ground truth to be wrong about,
and the almanac reports the flagship's sightline with its drift rate — at
day zero the accumulated drift is exactly nothing, so the rate is the story.

## The batteries the moons always had

Two hundred and fifty-six seeds now sweep the star (mass bounds, every
derivation monotone in mass — luminosity, habitable zone, brightening), the
anchor (orbit always inside the zone, Kepler III holding to the model card's
own formula, every rotation regime reachable, locked worlds never sprouting
a solar hour), and the neighborhood (counts, legal sky coordinates,
byte-identical regeneration); a fourth battery round-trips the alignment
dating inverse across seeds and latitudes to under a day. The campaign's own
machinery arrived battery-covered rather than promising coverage later.

## What it cost, and what it left

Two lab metrics (`brightening-per-gyr`, `alignment-drift-deg-per-kyr`) join
the census, which moves the schema and leaves the committed census fixtures
stale until the once-per-campaign AWS regeneration — deliberately shared
with Eclipse Seasons rather than paid twice. The followup register carries
the standstill handoff, a retrograde-mirror test, and the eventual
unification of the almanac's sightline display onto the committed fact. The
audit's ledger of residuals is settled: the aging star, the ground-level
alignments, and the verification batteries close here; the dated eclipse
belongs to Eclipse Seasons, where its deeper half lives.
