# The Cloister

When the dragon was given its mind, the mind it received was a borrowed one.
It had been cut for a people: six dimensions in a single cloth, half of them
describing an inner temperament — how boldly a thing meets danger, how long it
deliberates, how far ahead it plans — and half describing a *society* — how
authority is shaped, what earns standing, how wide the circle of "us" is drawn.
For the four goblinoid peoples the whole cloth fit, because each is at once a
mind and a polity. For a dragon it did not. A creature that keeps no company was
made to carry a politics anyway: a rank it holds over no one, a standing esteemed
by no one, an "us" narrowed almost to nothing — the last figure honest only
because it was almost the absence it should have been.

This campaign cuts the cloth in two. The temperament — threat-response,
deliberation, time-horizon — becomes the **mind vector**, carried by anything
that thinks. The politics — authority, status, in-group breadth — becomes the
**society vector**, carried only by the kinds that form a society: the settling
peoples, and no one else. A solitary mind holds the first and simply lacks the
second. Where the old model made the dragon assert a society it did not have,
the new one lets it be what it is — a self entire, with no polity to describe.

The split was meant to be felt only in the shape of the types, not in any world.
And it was. A settling people carries both vectors, with the same numbers it
always had, split across two homes; every reading a people ever produced comes
out unchanged. A dragon carries only its mind, and when some old machine still
asks it for a society — the voice that names its speech, the morphology of its
epithets — the question resolves to a baseline that was, by construction, the
very reading the dragon used to give. So the ledger of every world holds still,
and the dragons' frozen Draconic renders exactly as before, down to the byte.
The verification was not a promise but a measurement: the reference pages were
regenerated and found identical, because the thing that changed was not what the
world says but the grammar in which it is allowed to say it.

A discipline attends the cut. The society vector belongs to the settled and to
no one else, and a single invariant now guards that equivalence: any creature
carrying a politics must be a builder of communities, and every builder must
carry one — a solitary that smuggled in a society, or a people that lost its own,
is turned away at the gate. The lattice of capacities the previous campaign built
— to speak one must perceive, to perceive one must think — gains an orthogonal
axis beside it: to have a society, one must settle.

Nothing is placed yet; the dragon still walks no map. But its mind is now its
own, uncrossed by a society it never kept — the hermit in its cell, at last
described in the grammar of one. The noticing is still the next campaign; this
one only made sure that when the world finally notices the dragon, it will meet a
whole creature, and not a people wearing a dragon's shape.
