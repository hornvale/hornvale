# The Belonging

Five drives had turned a creature's attention to itself and to the ground it
stood on — its thirst and hunger and weariness, the cold of a cell, the dread of
a cursed place. But a creature felt nothing for the *others*. A foraging goblin
wandered as far from its village as any need drove it and never missed it. This
campaign added the sixth and last of the Temperament drives, affiliation, and
with it the creature first looked outward — to its own kind.

Two findings shaped the drive. The first is where it sits: **affiliation is the
first drive whose field is other agents.** The five before it read the self
(internal stocks) or the environment (fields over terrain); this one reads a
third thing, the presence of one's people. It is the root of a subtree —
attachment to *specific* others, status, conformity, mating — of which the
campaign took only the root. The second is its shape: **sociality has an
optimum**, not a monotone "more is better" — too isolated is lonely, too crowded
is stress — which is precisely thermal comfort's shape, a deviation from a
setpoint, with the field being how much of one's kind is near rather than the
temperature. So affiliation reused the flow-drive template thermal had
established: a gentle homeward pull, comfort-tier, that a thirsty or frightened
creature overrides and attends to only once its survival is secure.

The company a creature keeps is, in this first tier, proxied by its **home** — a
creature's home is its people. Loneliness rises with the distance it has strayed,
and its remedy is simply to head back. Modeling company as a *field* — proximity
to home — rather than as the perception of individual agents was a deliberate
choice, and it dodged a real hazard: reading other creatures' positions directly
is both a determinism trap (whose position, sampled when?) and a measurement trap
(scattered villagers would each read chronic loneliness and the world would look
sick). The population field a creature could affiliate at *any* village, and true
attachment to *particular* kin, wait in reserve, exactly as the wandering sun
waited behind The Slumber's coarse clock.

The interesting work, as ever, was where the new drive met the old machinery, and
it forced a genuine refinement. A creature stranded impossibly far from home —
past the reach of any plan back — must not read as *distressed*: it has not lost
something vital, it has merely relocated, and loneliness is a comfort, not a
matter of life. So the drive's pull is gated on reachability: within homing range
a lonely creature heads home and reads *Searching*, the ordinary seeking that is
not distress; beyond it the drive falls *dormant*, silent rather than despairing.
This is what lets a healthy world stay calm — a reachable home is always served,
so no one is chronically lonely — while leaving a genuinely stranded creature's
thirst, its real and mortal need, unmasked beneath a comfort that has quietly
stood down. The distinction is the whole of it: you can die of thirst you cannot
reach; you cannot die of a home you cannot find.

And so the drive is the gentlest of the six. It moves no creature that a stronger
need has claimed; it pulls only the sated and the safe, and only those within
sight of home, drawing them back the way a long day draws a body toward its own
door. It changed nothing a seed already recorded — no draw, no fact, and even the
possessed world's herder, who forages close to its village, never strays far
enough to feel it, so its transcript is unchanged. The pull waits for the creature
that wanders too far, and — when attachment and status and the whole subtree above
this root are one day built — for the day a creature will miss not just its home
but a *particular* face within it. With affiliation, the six drives of the
Temperament are complete: a creature that thirsts, shivers, tires, hungers, fears,
and, at last, belongs.
