# The Echo

*Common speaks; Common hears itself.*

Every campaign of the Self-Writing Book program so far taught the world to
say something. This one — inserted between The Predicates & the Grammar
and The Tongues, on an argument about timing — taught it to *listen*, and
proved the two directions are one machine. The Common grammar is now a
single construction inventory: an ordered list of surface parts (subject,
copula, determiner, complement, modifier tail) that one interpreter walks
forward into a sentence and another walks backward into the
`ClauseSpec` the sentence came from. The law that binds them is the
round-trip:

> `parse(realize(spec)) == spec`

and its corpus form: **every line The Book writes, The Book can read
back, byte-identically.** Both are standing tests now — the second joins
the program's gate, so any construction a future campaign adds must ship
with its reverse direction or the gate reddens. Comprehension can no
longer drift from production, structurally.

## Why now

The inventory is the smallest it will ever be — one frame, a modifier
tail, three construction-table fragments. Recasting the grammar as
bidirectional data cost one small campaign at this size; after The
Tongues multiplies realizers and the later campaigns grow the frame
stock, the same retrofit would cost proportionally more, forever. Only
Common ever needs to run backward: the generated tongues stay render-only
by design, because creature-to-creature transfer happens at the fact
level and the player boundary speaks Common.

## The parser a closed world buys

Parsing is the famously hard half of natural language, and the campaign's
quiet lesson is how much of that hardness evaporates inside a closed
world. There is no unknown-word problem: every content word in existence
was generated by the engine, so the parser receives the complete
complement lexicon as context and binds slots by longest match, not by
stemming. There is almost no ambiguity: what homophony the sound-change
engine produces lives in the generated tongues, never in Common. The
parser is a pure function — no draws, no wall clock — and total over what
the grammar generates, which is exactly the correctness it owes: the
inventory *defines* the language. Failure is typed and recountable in the
project's gap philosophy, and there are provably three ways to fail
(a fourth error variant died in review, proven unreachable by the
complement filter's own guarantees).

## Text as a knowledge carrier

The game-facing proof lives in the vessel: `tell Vebe is a planet with
two moons.` parses through the same grammar and lands in the possession
session's knowledge ledger as the facts the sentence carried. The
transfer law is pinned end to end — a fact rendered by the speaker,
transited as text, heard by the listener, survives with its surface
values exact. *Surface* values, deliberately: what transfers is what the
sentence says ("about 1.5"), not the full-precision quantity underneath —
the same boundary serialization quantization draws, and the honest
semantics for speech. Heard knowledge is likewise honest about its
provenance: it lives outside the ledger-derivable contract, because a
creature can tell you something false, and that is not a bug in the
listener.

The bridge deliberately not crossed: most creatures should not understand
"1.5" at all. Numeracy as a per-listener comprehension filter — a
quantity heard only down to the listener's numeral-system rung — is
banked as the owner's direction for a later campaign, the first purely
quantitative filter in the epistemic stack.

## What the reviews caught

Three defects died before merge, each to a review that measured: an error
variant proven unreachable and removed rather than left as untestable
surface; a transfer-law test that checked key presence where exact
surface values were available (the fix came back with mutation evidence —
both formatting branches now die when mutated); and a golden-report
re-pin that lagged its drifting commit until the final task's zero-drift
check refused to proceed past it. The whole campaign moved no artifact
byte: the realizer rewrite left every committed volume identical, which
was the refactor's safety rail and the merge's proof.
