# The Book Polish

*Two peoples should never read as one line typed twice.*

The self-writing-book program shipped its Reckoning-of-Years and esoteric
layers with two rough edges, both flagged and deliberately deferred at the
time. This campaign closes them, touching nothing else.

**The anonymous folk-line stutter.** Every folk-only culture's line in the
Reckoning opened with the same fixed, unattributed sentence — organized
cultures got a second line (their priesthood's count, their prophecy) to
tell them apart, but a folk-only culture never did. Wherever a world placed
two folk-only peoples back to back in one epoch, the committed text read
as an apparent duplicate:

> The sky has darkened, now and again.
> The sky has darkened, now and again.

It now names its own people, every time, at every rung — matching the
convention the Numbered and Predictive lines already used:

> Among the Shteozqae, the sky has darkened, now and again.
> Among the Jjajjjo, the sky has darkened, now and again.

**The dangling initiated line.** The esoteric layer's continuation —
what the initiated know beyond what the folk say — rendered as a bare
fragment, fine inside the vessel's turn-by-turn flow but floating with no
antecedent in the CLI's static dump. It now states its own subject and
agrees in number with its count, mirroring the exoteric claim it answers:

> Vebe has two moons, as the initiated count.

Both fixes are closed-string surface changes only: no new draws, no new
facts, no mechanism. The corpus law's round-trip is extended to the
attributed folk line; the initiated line carries no parse obligation and
never did. The committed Book's diff is exactly the touched lines' own
text — nothing added, nothing removed, every other section byte-identical.
