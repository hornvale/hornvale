# The Bane

The Dread gave every creature the same fear, and The Mettle let it feel that fear
more or less keenly — but it was always fear of the *same things*. A cursed grove,
a molten waste, a frozen one: hazards, feared alike by all. Yet what a creature
dreads is part of what it *is*. A cold-adapted mammoth fears a heat that a warm
bugbear shrugs off; a fire-drake is untroubled by flame that would kill a treant.
This campaign gave fear a **niche** — each creature weights the *kinds* of hazard
by its own nature — and in doing so completed a trilogy: diet, threat, and
sociality, the three niches by which a creature is what it eats, what it fears, and
whom it keeps.

The design came from lifting three drives to their common shape. Hunger's diet
niche, danger's threat niche, and The Mettle's boldness are, abstracted, one
thing: a *signed valuation over environmental fields* — a charge. Food is a charge
that is always positive, and a creature climbs toward it. Threat is a charge that
may be either sign: like charges repel, and a mortal flees the uncanny; opposite
charges attract, and a fire elemental is drawn toward the flame. Boldness is a
scalar on the whole charge. Seen this way, the threat niche is nothing new — it is
hunger's own dot product, `niche · field`, run over hazard axes instead of
resource ones. The code has been reaching for this shape all along; the same
gradient-walk serves food, comfort, and fear, once you see they are the same walk.

The threat itself split into three axes the world already provided — the uncanny,
the heat, the cold — and, rather than author a fear for every creature, the niche
was *derived* from what each creature already is. A creature dreads the thermal
extreme *away* from its comfort: the mammoth, whose temperature niche sits deep in
the cold, weights heat heavily and cold hardly at all; the bugbear, warm-loving,
the reverse. And the uncanny it reads from a creature's very substance — a
metabolizing mortal fears the eldritch, while an elemental, being itself a thing of
elements, *is* eldritch and feels no dread of it. So the whole niche falls out of
the temperature niche and the metabolic class, two data every creature already
carried; not a line of it needed authoring.

There is a shore this campaign kept to the near side of. A charge may go negative —
a creature *drawn* toward the very hazard others flee, the moth to its flame, and
one day the hunter to its prey. That is attraction, and it is the same far shore
The Mettle left unsailed at the reckless end of its dial: both are the negative
charge, the fear that has become desire, and both wait together for the food web to
give a creature something worth being drawn toward. So a fire-drake, in this
telling, is not yet *drawn* to fire — it is merely *fearless* of it, its heat-fear
weighted to nothing. The step from fearless to drawn is a sign, and the sign is
reserved.

As ever, the change reached only where it should. The niche derives from data
already authored, draws no seed, and touches no save; genesis is byte-for-byte
unchanged, and so is the possessed hobgoblin's transcript, for it walks temperate
ground and meets neither cursed place nor killing cold — every hazard axis reads
zero beneath it, and zero dotted with any niche is zero. The dread the goblinoids
now feel differs by kind only where the world turns extreme, which is exactly where
it should. And the exotic niches — the dragon unafraid of its element, the xorn
unafraid of the strange — wait authored and correct, for the day such a creature
first opens its eyes as an agent and walks a world that, at last, it fears in its
own particular way.
