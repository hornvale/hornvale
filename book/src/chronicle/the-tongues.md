# The Tongues

*The same fact, and it does not sound the same twice.*

Until now every sentence the world spoke came out in Common. This
campaign — the metaplan's third, first after the inserted Echo — gives
each placed people its own voice. At the end of every volume of The Book,
the peoples now state who they are in their own languages:

> Saa We Vavako. *(in the goblin tongue: "The Vavako are goblins.")*
> Ddenke Babo. *(in the hobgoblin tongue: "The Ddenke are hobgoblins.")*

Nothing in those lines was authored. The goblins' language drew a
verb-initial constituent order and an overt copula — *Saa*, a word built
from the goblin phonology by the same machinery that builds every goblin
word. The hobgoblins drew zero-copula predication, so their sentence is
two bare nominals, which is how Russian or Arabic says the same thing.
Word order and copula presence are seeded draws on three new permanent
streams, weighted by the real cross-linguistic frequencies (SOV and SVO
dominate, as they do on Earth), and drawn *per species* — because on
Earth, word order is historically contingent, not a consequence of a
people's psychology, and pretending otherwise would ship astrology as
science. The one derivation the campaign refused is the one that would
have been fake.

The machinery is one parametric realizer over the grammar the Echo made:
a `TongueClause` (a subject already in surface form, a complement as a
*concept* to look up in the speaker's own lexicon) walks out through the
tongue's drawn parameters. The realizer is render-only — Common remains
the only language that parses, by design — and it renders honestly or
not at all: if the speaker's lexicon lacks a word, the whole sentence
gaps, carrying the lexicon's own recountable reason.

Which is the campaign's second delivery: the gap made visible. No culture
in any world holds a *planet* concept — a pre-Copernican people calls its
world "the ground" and has no word for the class of wandering lights it
belongs to — so no tongue can render the Book's planet sentence. The
Book now says so, per tongue, in a coverage report: `goblin: gap — planet
(no entry in this lexicon)`. That line is the first working sliver of the
chorus to come: a culture describes the world only to the depth it
understands, and the depth is *measured*, not asserted. When the
epistemic filters arrive, they will deepen a mechanism that already
exists rather than introduce one.

Blast radius, measured at the close: zero. No concept was added (the
Predicates campaign's lesson — a concept-add can displace existing
lexemes — was deliberately not re-run), so every lexicon in every world
is byte-identical; the new streams perturb no existing draw; the Common
lines of the Book did not move by one byte. The tongues arrived beside
everything else, touching nothing.
