# The Haunt

Every fear before this one lived in the present tense. The Dread taught a creature to
feel the danger of the ground beneath it; The Bane taught it to feel each kind of
danger by its own measure; The Mettle set how loudly; The Alarm let one creature catch
another's dread. All of them answer the question *what is frightening here, now.* This
campaign asks a different one — *where was I frightened before* — and lets the answer
bend a creature's path. It gives fear a memory.

The shape was waiting in a function the world already had. A thirsty creature does not
wander blind; it remembers the water it has stood in and plans toward the nearest such
place it knows, a belief re-derived every moment from the plain record of where it has
been. `believed_water` is that memory of relief. The Haunt builds its opposite twin —
`believed_hazard`, the memory of dread: among the ground a creature has crossed, the
cells where its own fear once crossed the threshold to act. Where the one is a single
place sought, the other is a whole set shunned; where the one pulls a plan toward
itself, the other pushes a plan away. The asymmetry is the point. You drink at the one
nearest spring, but you give a wide berth to *every* bad place you remember.

That asymmetry hides a deeper one. Water is permanent: the spring a creature remembers
is the spring that is still there, so `believed_water` can never be wrong. But danger
is a passing thing — the predator moves on, the season turns, the alarm fades — so a
memory of danger can be *stale*. `believed_hazard` is the drive layer's first belief
that can be false, the small individual seed of every phobia and superstition the
world will later grow. The world corrects it the way experience does: a cell is
remembered as dangerous only if the creature's *most recent* visit there was a
frightened one, so standing safely on old bad ground disproves the fear and clears it.
For now, while the danger a creature remembers is the slow danger of the terrain
itself, the disproof rarely comes — the haunted ravine is still a ravine. The fear that
truly outlives its cause waits for a later campaign to remember the dangers that pass.

The memory had to enter behaviour somewhere, and the first instinct — to let a creature
*feel* remembered dread standing on old bad ground — turned out to be nearly empty. A
creature already senses the danger of the cell beneath it and the cells a step away; to
also *remember* the danger of ground it can already sense adds nothing. Fear's memory is
worth something only where its reach exceeds its senses — and that is the planner, the
very place `believed_water` does its work. So remembered danger became a weight on the
map a creature plans over: a step onto shunned ground costs more, and the path-finder,
seeking the cheapest route to water or home, bends *around* the haunted cell of its own
accord — provided a detour is cheaper than the dread. The creature no longer blunders
toward the bad place and flinches at its edge, tick after tick; it plans its whole
journey to avoid it from the start. And because the added cost is finite, a creature
with nowhere else to drink still braves the ravine when the thirst grows dire enough —
the flinch is a preference, never a cage. Present danger is still the flow drive's to
feel; the past is carried in the plan.

The campaign expected to change nothing on the canonical world, and was wrong in an
instructive way. Every fear before it had stayed invisible on seed 42 because the
settled peoples, sat on their good ground, are never frightened at all — an empty memory
changes no plan. But the world has grown wilder than the peoples: since the fauna were
made to walk, seed 42's beasts roam genuinely dangerous ground, and *they* remember it.
So remembered danger is the first fear that does not sleep on the canonical world — a
handful of wild creatures now give the bad places a wide berth, and the record of a walk
through their country shifts by a few stirred souls. That was not a regression to be
hidden but the feature drawing its first breath, and the choice was made deliberately:
let the world change by exactly the intended amount, and prove that nothing else moved.
The peoples remain as they were; the beasts have learned to shun the ground that
frightened them; and the map a creature carries now holds not only where the water is,
but where the fear was.
