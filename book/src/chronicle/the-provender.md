# The Provender

Creatures in Hornvale thirsted, shivered, tired, and slept — but never hungered.
They did not eat. And the substrate for food was already laid, unused: every
cell carries a net primary productivity, a proxy for its standing biomass, and
every species carries a *diet* — an authored vector of weights over resource
axes (photosynthate, plant forage, animal prey, detritus, mineral). The world
grew the food and named who ate what; nothing yet connected the two. This
campaign added the fourth drive, hunger, and closed that circuit.

The result that shaped the design came from a pass through ideonomy: **hunger is
not a new kind of drive — it is the thirst homeostat with the niche as its
dial.** A creature depletes a resource stock by living and refills it by contact
with a source; thirst and hunger differ only in *which* resource, *what* source
serves it, and *how fast* it burns. So hunger reuses thirst's machinery
wholesale. Its urgency is the same temperature-coupled path integral
[The Kindling](./the-kindling.md) built for thirst — a second consumer of the
very same integral, so a hot-blooded creature in the heat burns, and hungers,
faster. Its ceiling is survival, like thirst and unlike comfort or fatigue:
starving is lethal. Where it diverges from thirst is in *space*. Water is binary
and sought by belief; food is a graded field, and a hungry creature climbs its
gradient — grazing toward richer ground and eating in place once the cell beneath
it is productive enough.

The niche is the whole trick. A creature's diet is a continuous *mix* over the
resource axes — an omnivore is half forage, half prey; an autotroph is pure
photosynthate — and that mix already *is* the creature's position on the
autotroph-heterotroph spectrum. So the drive reads the niche and never branches
on a diet type. There is no "herbivore versus carnivore" switch to write; the
food-value of a cell for a creature is simply its niche dotted with what the cell
provides. The material axes read the cell's productivity; the photosynthate axis
reads *light* — the sun above the horizon — so an autotroph is fed by day and
starves at night, its feeding cycle nesting inside the wake cycle
[The Wakeful Sun](./the-wakeful-sun.md) already computes. And the gate on whether
a creature hungers at all is not its metabolism but its niche crossed with the
world: a creature hungers for an axis when its niche weights that axis *and* a
reachable source provides it. "Ametabolic, so it does not eat" falls out as a
consequence — a construct carries no food niche a source serves — rather than
being written as a special case.

Three futures are therefore correct-by-construction the moment such a creature
becomes an agent, though none is built: an autotroph fed by light, a lithovore
that grazes mineral, a scavenger that reads detritus. Each is a reserved seam in
the food-value sum, waiting only for a people that weights that axis. What ships
is the omnivore's half — grazing toward productivity, eating where the ground is
rich — because the goblinoids and the beasts that walk the world today are
foragers and grazers, not yet hunters. Active predation, the food web, the
prey-cycles that turn one creature's hunger into another's danger, are deferred
to the trophic engine that this drive is the first instance of: one
resource-homeostat, many instantiations — thirst, hunger, and one day mana, and
the exotic trophs down to the faith-fed god who starves when forgotten.

The change reached only where it should. Genesis is untouched — food-value is a
derived read over climate and niche, drawing no seed and storing no state, so
every almanac, map, and census is byte-identical. Only the possessed world moved:
its creatures now graze and eat, so the possession galleries gained their meals,
and the herder you walk beside no longer merely *seems content* — as its hunger
climbs through the afternoon, it *grows restless*, and later goes down to graze.
The world had always grown the food. Now something is finally eating it.
