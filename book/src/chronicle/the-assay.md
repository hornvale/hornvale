# The Assay

Every creature in the world is weighed twice. Once for its body — a mass in
kilograms, read straight from the old bestiary, honest to the gram. And once
for its might: a single number, *potency*, the magic in a thing that lets a
dragon shrug off a cold a goblin must obey. Mass had always named its source.
Might never had. It was the one dimension in the whole species model set down
by eye — a red dragon `0.95`, white and black alike at `0.85`, a treant `0.6` —
chosen because they *felt* right and ordered the world plausibly enough that no
one had looked twice.

This campaign looked twice. An assay is the test that tells true metal from
base by measuring it against a known standard, and the standard had been there
all along, in the same corpus the mass came from: the Challenge Rating, the one
number the bestiary publishes as *how much this thing is to be feared*. Potency
is now that rating over thirty — the published ceiling, so the whole scale from
house-cat to tarrasque falls onto the interval the sovereignty floor reads —
and the three chromatic dragons at last rank as the source ranks them: red
above black above white, not two of them tied and all three crowded against the
top.

The change moved nothing. Not a byte of any world shifted, because the mighty
do not yet walk the placed roster — they wait in the biosphere for the day the
menagerie is set upon the ground, and only then will a red dragon's slightly
lighter buffering make it, correctly, a shade less rare. An assay does not
forge new metal; it only reads what the metal already is.

But the assay was the smallest piece of a larger question, and the question is
the point. The model draws a hard line: four peoples who think and speak and
settle, and everything else — dragons among them — filed under *fauna*,
mindless and mute by the mere absence of a row. Yet a dragon is neither mindless
nor mute; it is *solitary*. The line we had drawn was never between the sapient
and the beast but between the communal and the alone, and we had mistaken the
one for the other. The entity-component substrate was built precisely to
abolish such lines — a creature *is* the components it carries, no more — and
the last inheritance tier had survived inside it, unexamined, calling itself
*personhood*. Personhood is not a kind of thing; it is a region of a space, and
a dragon stands in the region next door: a mind and a tongue, kept in solitude.
What language becomes for a creature with no one to drift alongside — a tongue
frozen where a people's would splinter into dialects — is the country this
program means to enter. This campaign only assayed the coin to spend there.
