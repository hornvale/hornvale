# The Doctrine

*The folk watch the sky. The priesthood keeps its books.*

Through the fifth campaign, every culture in the chorus spoke with one
voice. This campaign — the metaplan's sixth — splits it: wherever a
culture's flagship settlement committed an organized cult (a shaman caste
in its derived social structure, already on the ledger since religion's
genesis), the Book now carries a second account, the institution's,
printed under its own heading after the folk's:

> **As the Vavako tell it.**
> Vebe is the earth.
> The day returns because the sky must be crossed.
>
> **As the priesthood of the Vavako teach it.**
> Vebe is the earth.
> The moons are counted and known to the priesthood.
> The moons cross because Soevvae strides the sky, slowly.
> The day returns because Wowako strides the sky, briskly.

The folk hold a path cosmology; their priesthood teaches an agentive one,
names its gods, and — the line doing the most work — *knows things the
folk do not*. The goblin folk cannot count the moons (a diurnal people
with ordinary night eyes); their priesthood, keeping records across
generations, can. The Book renders that asymmetry as a claim of
knowledge, not the knowledge itself: **counted and known**, no number.
This is the calendar-priesthood mechanism made text — authority that
rests precisely on holding what others cannot check.

Nothing about the priesthood is authored per culture, and nothing
anywhere models deception. The doctrinal account is the folk account
passed through four global transformations: capability rises (records
beat memory), the schema priors reweight by a fixed *mediation* column
(explanations that require institutional mediation and resist folk
verification weigh heavier — agency, moral debt, essence; the
self-evident cycle and path weigh lighter), the explanatory-monomania
dial tightens (orthodoxy), and the presiding high god takes the agent
slot when its worshipped rhythm fits. The distortion is a **selection
bias**: the institution sincerely selects the stories that pass through
it. No theory of mind, no lies — and the machinery is field-checked by a
standing law asserting the doctrine's parameters differ from the folk's
in exactly four places and no more.

Where the two accounts differ, a derived four-state map classifies the
difference and the Book obeys it. Where folk could check and the
accounts still differ, disclosure is mandatory — the doctrine paragraph
must carry *— though the folk say…* — and the renderer fails loudly
rather than let that annotation silently vanish. Where folk cannot
check, doctrine speaks alone, confident: the mystery cell, where
authority honestly accrues. At today's floor the mystery cell is where
every real difference lives — the measured worlds hold no genuinely
contested claim yet, a structural fact the suite pins with a canary that
reddens the day one appears.

And the Book itself became reader-relative, at its honest floor. The
committed edition is exoteric: the knowledge-claim without the value. A
reader whose own knowledge already contains the fact — the vessel's
transfer seam is the intended source — is shown one line more:

> — two, as the initiated count.

The value comes from the ledger and nowhere else (the suite proves the
source by mutation), so the initiated edition invents nothing; it
confirms. The Book stops being one text for all readers and becomes a
function of what its reader already knows — the game seam the program
has been walking toward since the first sentence.

What waits: the tongues rendering the teaching with evidential morphology
— *taught-that* as grammar — at the seventh campaign, which will read
this campaign's conflict states as its evidential sources; and the day a
second revealable fact exists, when the loud guards this campaign left
behind will demand the new formulas be authored deliberately rather than
inherited silently.
