# The Vessel Stitch

*You consult the Book, and the Book knows what you have written in your
margin.*

The self-writing-book program ended with two seams deliberately left
open toward the game: the doctrine campaign's initiated lines waited for
a real reader whose knowledge could unlock them, and the diachronic
campaign's time axis waited to be read from inside the world it
measures. This small campaign stitches both shut with one verb each:

> `> consult`
> The Reckoning, at day 0.
> The sky keeps no dates to number.
> The Book holds more for the initiated.
>
> `> write Vebe is a planet with two moons.`
> Written in the margin.
>
> `> consult`
> The Reckoning, at day 0.
> The sky keeps no dates to number.
> — two, as the initiated count.

`consult` reads the world's Book at the possessed session's own day —
wait a hundred years in the vessel and the same verb renders the
hundredth-year reckoning, priesthood, prophecy, and margin included.
And `write` — the transfer verb the Echo shipped as `tell`, renamed here
at the owner's word — puts what you have heard into your copy's margin,
the program's own device turned toward the reader. What you have written
is what the Book confirms to you.

The one semantic tooth is the old Echo point, now playable: heard is not
true. Write a wrong count — "nine moons" — and the margin still opens to
you, but the Book answers with *its* number, never yours. This is
structural, not policed: only the fact's key ever crosses the seam from
your knowledge to the Book; the value the initiated line speaks is read
from the ledger at the moment of consulting. The Book is an authority,
not a mirror.

Nothing else moved. No draws, no facts, no artifacts, no census — the
committed Book is byte-identical, because `consult` is a live rendering
the way possession itself is. The Casement's in-browser vessel gains
both verbs at its next deploy for free. The recorded next step is the
one this stitch makes obvious: a verb that earns initiation by
*observation* — standing under the night sky the possession already
narrates, and counting.
