# The Disposition

Six drives had accreted, one campaign at a time, onto a single function. Each new
drive left `arbitrate` — the seam that turns a creature's needs into an action —
a little heavier at its edges: a psychology dial here, a wake-gate flag there,
until it took nine arguments and wore a `#[allow]` to excuse the crowd. Every
campaign that added to it had noted, in passing, that the loose scalars wanted
gathering; none had stopped to do it, because the drive in front of them was more
interesting than the housekeeping behind. This campaign, with the drive set
complete, stopped and did it.

The gathering is a small idea with a clean edge. Of the nine things `arbitrate`
took, four were not the *situation* a creature was in but the *disposition* it
brought to reading that situation — the two psychology dials that weight one drive
against another (how much a creature deliberates, how far it looks ahead) and the
two momentary states that gate them (whether it has given up, whether it is
awake). These are exactly what make the same perception and the same needs yield
a different decision in two different creatures, or in one creature at two
different moments. They are the *disposition*, and now they travel together, in a
struct of that name, leaving the function to take what a decision actually needs:
what the creature perceives, what it wants, the frame of the world around it, and
the disposition it decides with. Nine arguments became six, and the apology for
the crowd came off.

Nothing about the world changed — that was the whole discipline of it. A
consolidation like this earns its keep only if it is provably nothing more than a
consolidation, and the proof is byte-identity: the function reads
`disposition.latency` where it read a bare `latency`, packs no value differently,
reorders no evaluation, and so every world, every almanac, and — for the first
time in five campaigns — every possession transcript comes out bit-for-bit
unchanged. The test of it was the test suite itself: a hundred arbitration cases,
each asserting exactly what it asserted before, all of them still green. A single
moved expectation would have meant the refactor had quietly changed something and
was wrong; none moved.

It is the quietest campaign in the chronicle, and deliberately so. But the drive
layer is where the reserved engines — the trophic web, the chronobiology stack,
the threat and affiliation niches — will one day be built, and each of them will
reach for `arbitrate` again. They will find it composed: its inputs named, its
disposition gathered, its apology retired. The housekeeping the six drives kept
deferring is done, and the next campaign to add to this function will add to a
tidy one.
